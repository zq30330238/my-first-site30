The Phantom Troupe: Spider's Web in Hunter x Hunter

April 7, 2025 · 6 min read

Origins of the Spider

The Phantom Troupe, also known as the Spiders, is a notorious group of thieves in the world of Hunter x Hunter. Their origins trace back to the dark streets of Meteor City, a lawless wasteland where the weak are prey. The Troupe was formed by Chrollo Lucilfer, a charismatic and enigmatic leader who sought to create a family bound not by blood but by shared purpose. Each member wears a numbered tattoo of a twelve-legged spider, symbolizing that if one leg is cut off, the others will continue to function. This philosophy drives their relentless pursuit of wealth and power, often leaving a trail of destruction.

The Spider's creed is simple: if a member dies, the remaining eleven will avenge them. This blind loyalty is both their greatest strength and their fatal flaw.

Key Members and Their Roles

The Phantom Troupe consists of thirteen members, each with a unique ability and role. Chrollo Lucilfer (leader, #0) can steal Nen abilities with his Bandit's Secret. Feitan (#2) is an expert torturer and uses pain to fuel his Rising Sun ability. Nobunaga Hazama (#1) is a swordsman whose En technique can cut anything within range. Pakunoda (#9) can read memories through touch, a vital intel-gathering tool. Shizuku (#8) uses Blinky, a vacuum cleaner-like Nen beast that she can command to suck up anything (except blood). Kortopi (#12) can conjure exact replicas of objects. These diverse skills make the Troupe a devastating force in both combat and heists.

Lore Significance and Thematic Impact

The Phantom Troupe is more than a group of villains; they represent the dark side of human desire in the Hunter x Hunter world. Their actions challenge the protagonists' morality, forcing characters like Kurapika to confront the cost of revenge. The Troupe's backstory in Meteor City highlights cycles of violence and survival, adding depth to their ruthless behavior. Their role in the Yorknew City arc is pivotal, showcasing how Nen abilities and strategic planning can create near-invincible threats. Even after losing members, the Spider adapts, symbolizing resilience and the persistence of organized crime in a world where power often trumps justice. Fans continue to debate whether the Troupe is truly evil or just a product of their environment, making them one of anime's most compelling antagonistic forces.
