Blang traditional attire is characterized by dark indigo-dyed fabrics with striking silver ornamentation. Women favor cylindrical skirts with embroidered hems and collarless jackets adorned with silver studs and beads.

Key Features of Blang Attire

  • Dark indigo-dyed fabric as the primary textile
  • Cylindrical ankle-length skirts with patterned hem bands
  • Silver studs and beads sewn onto jacket collars and front panels
  • Colorful beaded necklaces and earrings in geometric arrangements
  • Mens front-fastening jackets with decorative silver buttons

Traditional Garments

Women wear collarless, tight-fitting short jackets in dark blue or black, fastened with silver buttons, paired with a long cylindrical skirt reaching the ankles with embroidered bands at the hem. Men wear collarless front-fastening jackets with wide-leg trousers and a cloth belt.

Headwear and Adornments

Women wrap their hair in black or dark blue cloth turbans, sometimes with floral decorations inserted at the temples. Some Blang women shave their eyebrows as a traditional beauty standard.

Embroidery and Decorative Arts

Blang embroidery is concentrated on skirt hems and jacket edges, featuring geometric diamond and zigzag patterns in red, yellow, and white threads against dark fabric. Beadwork is more prominent than thread embroidery.

The Blang, one of Chinas oldest tea-growing peoples, often incorporate tea-leaf motifs into their embroidery patterns as a quiet homage to their ancestral livelihood.

Color Symbolism

Dark indigo blue and black form the base, with bright red, yellow, white, and green used for embroidery detailing and bead trim.

Festival Attire

During the Water Splashing Festival and the Gannian Festival, women wear their most heavily silver-ornamented jackets, multiple strands of bead necklaces, and full-length embroidered skirts.

Modern Influence and Preservation

Blang indigo dyeing techniques are maintained by village cooperatives in Yunnan.

Did You Know?

The Blang people have a unique tradition where girls in some communities traditionally shave their eyebrows, considering it a mark of beauty.
