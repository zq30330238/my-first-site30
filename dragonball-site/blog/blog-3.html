Team Synergy in Dragon Ball FighterZ: Build the Perfect Squad

April 8, 2025 · 6 min read

Why Team Synergy Matters

Dragon Ball FighterZ is a 3v3 tag-team fighter where success depends on more than individual skill. The best players build teams where every character supports the others. Assist moves, DHC (Delayed Hyper Combo) paths, and anchor strength determine whether your squad crumbles or dominates. A well-synergized team lets you convert stray hits into massive damage, escape pressure, and lock down opponents with relentless offense.

Think of each slot as a role: point (first character), mid (second), and anchor (last). The point should have strong neutral and mix-ups. The mid needs good assists and DHC synergy. The anchor must work with limited resources and have comeback potential. Ignoring these roles often leads to wasted meter and awkward combos.

Building a Balanced Team

Start by choosing your point character. Look for fast normals, air mobility, and low-commitment options. Examples: Kid Buu, Bardock, or GT Goku. Your point should be able to open up opponents without relying heavily on assists, though assists help.

Next, pick a mid character with a versatile assist. The best assists cover multiple angles (e.g., beam assists like SS Goku's Kamehameha or hybrid assists like Yamcha's Wolf Fang Fist). Your mid should also DHC cleanly into your anchor's supers. Test combos where you tag from mid to anchor mid-combo--if it drops, find another option.

Your anchor is the last line of defense. This character needs good solo tools, a fast level 3 super, and comeback mechanics. Top anchors include Z Broly, Base Vegeta, and SS Vegeta. Avoid characters that rely on assists to function--once your anchor is alone, they must hold their own.

Pro Tip: Always save Sparking Blast for your anchor if possible. Activating Sparking mid-combo gives you full meter and huge damage, turning a desperate situation into a win. Practice corner combos with your anchor so you can kill any character with two touches.

Top Three Team Compositions

1. Shell Team (Kid Buu / SS Goku / SS Vegeta)
Kid Buu's chaotic neutral and great air normals pair with SS Goku's beam assist for easy blockstrings. SS Vegeta's assist locks down opponents, allowing Kid Buu to run mix-ups. DHC path: Kid Buu super into Goku's warp Kamehameha into Vegeta's Final Flash.

2. Grappler Core (Z Broly / Cooler / Base Vegeta)
Z Broly's armor and command grabs crush weak defense. Cooler's rapid fire assist helps Broly close distance. Base Vegeta's loop combos and strong level 3 anchor make this team lethal once you land a hit.

3. Rushdown Momentum (Bardock / Yamcha / Tien)
Bardock's lariat and fake mix-ups force reactions. Yamcha's blockstring assist extends pressure. Tien's volley ball super works in any DHC route, and his level 3 does massive damage even when low on health.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't stack three characters that all want to be point. You'll end up with weak assists and no anchor. Also, avoid characters with poor DHC compatibility--if a character's super knocks the opponent fullscreen and the next character can't follow up, you waste meter. Test every combo path in training mode before taking your team online.

Finally, remember that team synergy evolves with patches. Watch high-level tournaments (like EVO or CEO) to see what top players run. The meta shifts, but the principles of assist coverage, neutral tools, and anchor strength remain constant.
