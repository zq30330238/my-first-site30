Android 18

Cyborg Fighter / Krillin's Wife

Android 18, originally a human girl named Lazuli before being kidnapped and experimented on by Dr. Gero, is a remarkably nuanced character in Dragon Ball: a former villain turned loving wife and mother without losing her edge as a fierce combatant.Android 18, originally a human girl named Lazuli before being kidnapped and experimented on by Dr. Gero, is a remarkably nuanced character in Dragon Ball: a former villain turned loving wife and mother without losing her edge as a fierce combatant. Along with her twin brother Android 17, she was forcibly remodeled at the cellular level by Gero's biomechanical engineering and equipped with an infinite energy reactor that grants her unlimited stamina. Unlike the purely mechanical Androids 19 and 20, 18 is a cybernetically enhanced human who retained her free will and emotions, which ultimately led her to rebel against Gero's programming. During the Android Saga, 18 terrorized the Z-Fighters alongside her brother, effortlessly defeating Vegeta in their first encounter and humiliating the proud Saiyan prince. She later became the unwilling absorption target for Cell, an experience that nearly cost her life. After being rescued by Krillin's mercy, he destroyed the remote control that could have deactivated her, choosing love over strategy, 18's cold exterior gradually melted. Their unlikely romance became one of the series' most enduring relationships, producing a daughter named Marron. As a fighter, Android 18 combines infinite energy with cold precision and tremendous power: during the Tournament of Power, she fought on par with Super Saiyan-level opponents and formed a devastating tag-team with her brother 17, using their Accel Dance combination attack. Beyond combat, 18's sharp wit and deadpan humor provide a refreshing contrast to the earnest intensity of the Saiyan-dominated cast. Her character arc, from weapon of destruction to devoted mother who still enjoys breaking ribs in tournaments, illustrates that redemption does not require abandoning one's strength or personality. ...
