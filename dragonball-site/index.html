Son Goku

Kakarot, Super Saiyan God, Ultra Instinct. The greatest warrior in Universe 7 with mastery of multiple godly forms.

Son Goku Guides

Vegeta

Prince of all Saiyans, Ultra Ego. Rival to Goku who achieved power through sheer pride and relentless training.

Vegeta Guides

Gohan

Son of Goku, Beast Form, Ultimate. Possesses the highest latent potential of any character in the series.

Gohan Guides

Piccolo

Namekian Warrior, Orange Form. Former demon king turned Earth's greatest protector alongside Goku.

Piccolo Guides

Frieza

The most feared tyrant in Universe 7. From Namek saga villain to Tournament of Power ally, Black Frieza transformation.

Frieza Guides

Cell

Perfect life form created by Dr. Gero. Absorbed Androids 17 and 18 to achieve his perfect form and host the Cell Games.

Cell Guides

Majin Buu

Primordial force of destruction awakened by Bibidi. Multiple forms from Fat Buu to Kid Buu, each with unique absorption abilities.

Majin Buu Guides

Trunks

Son of Vegeta and Bulma. Time-traveling hero who warned of the Androids and achieved Super Saiyan Rage.

Trunks Guides

Bulma

Earth's smartest inventor and Goku's oldest friend. Creator of the Dragon Radar and Capsule Corp heir.

Bulma Guides

Krillin

Goku's best friend and strongest pure human. Destructo Disc master and tactical genius of the Z-Fighters.

Krillin Guides

Beerus

God of Destruction of Universe 7. The being who introduced Super Saiyan God and Ultra Instinct to Goku.

Beerus Guides

Whis

Angel attendant to Beerus and martial arts teacher. The fastest being in Universe 7 with Ultra Instinct mastery.

Whis Guides

Broly

The Legendary Super Saiyan with limitless growing power. From berserker villain to powerful ally trained by Goku and Vegeta.

Broly Guides

Jiren

Strongest warrior of Universe 11. A mortal who surpassed his own God of Destruction through absolute strength and willpower.

Jiren Guides

Tien Shinhan

Three-eyed descendant of the alien Triclops race. Master of the Tri-Beam, Solar Flare, and Multi-Form techniques.

Tien Shinhan Guides

Yamcha

Former desert bandit turned Z-Fighter. Spirit Ball and Wolf Fang Fist master who fought alongside the team from the very beginning.

Yamcha Guides

Master Roshi

The Turtle Hermit who trained Goku and Krillin. Creator of the Kamehameha and master of the Evil Containment Wave.

Master Roshi Guides

Android 18

Former Red Ribbon android turned loving wife of Krillin. Infinite energy reactor grants unlimited stamina in battle.

Android 18 Guides

Goten

Youngest son of Goku. Achieved Super Saiyan at age 7 and mastered Fusion Dance with Trunks to form Gotenks.

Goten Guides

Chi-Chi

Daughter of Ox-King and wife of Goku. A skilled martial artist who trained Goten and manages the Son family household.

Chi-Chi Guides

Chiaotzu

Small but mighty martial artist with powerful psychic abilities like telekinesis and paralysis. Trained alongside Tien Shinhan under Master Shen at the Crane School before both saw the error of their ways. Chiaotzu sacrificed himself against Nappa in a desperate attempt to save his friends, detonating himself in an explosion that proved futile against the Saiyan's might. Though sidelined in later arcs due to power scaling, he remains Tien's inseparable lifelong companion and a loyal Z-Fighter.

Chiaotzu Guides

Launch

A unique woman with a split personality triggered by sneezing. Her blue-haired form is sweet, gentle, and kind-hearted, while her blonde form is aggressive, gun-toting, and criminal. Blonde Launch loves Tien Shinhan and often pursues him, though he remains focused on training. She lived with Master Roshi and helped around Kame House before mysteriously disappearing in DBZ (Toriyama admitted he simply forgot about her). Her unpredictable nature made her one of the series' most memorable early characters.

Launch Guides

Oolong

A shapeshifting anthropomorphic pig who attended Shapeshifting Academy before being expelled for stealing the teacher's panties. Though cowardly and lecherous by nature, Oolong has proven himself useful on numerous occasions, using his transformation ability (limited to 5 minutes per shift) to assist Goku and friends. His most heroic moment came when he intercepted Emperor Pilaf's first wish for world domination by selfishly wishing for a pair of panties, inadvertently saving the world.

Oolong Guides

Pu'ar

A small floating blue cat-like creature and Yamcha's loyal best friend. Unlike Oolong, Pu'ar graduated from Shapeshifting Academy with honors and can maintain transformations indefinitely. Pu'ar has saved Yamcha's life multiple times, most notably transforming into scissors to cut off Goku's tail during his Great Ape rampage at Pilaf's castle. Gentle, loyal, and surprisingly capable, Pu'ar stays by Yamcha's side through all his highs and lows.

Pu'ar Guides

Emperor Pilaf

A diminutive blue imp-like creature who dreams of world domination. Pilaf was the very first villain Goku ever faced and the one who accidentally introduced the concept of the Dragon Balls to the story. Despite his grand ambitions and surprisingly advanced technology (including piloted mech suits), Pilaf's plans are always thwarted by Goku and company, often through comedic misfortune. In Dragon Ball Super, he and his gang accidentally wished themselves into children using the Dragon Balls, becoming recurring comic relief.

Emperor Pilaf Guides

Shu

A humanoid fox-dog creature who serves as Emperor Pilaf's loyal henchman alongside Mai. Shu dresses in ninja garb and operates Pilaf's various mechs and vehicles. Though completely incompetent as a villain, his loyalty to Pilaf never wavers. In Dragon Ball Super, after being wished into a child alongside Pilaf and Mai, Shu continues serving his diminutive master, often getting dragged into schemes doomed to fail.

Shu Guides

Mai

Pilaf's tall, dark-haired female henchwoman who operates his technology and carries out his schemes. Originally a competent but comedic antagonist, Mai's role evolved dramatically in Dragon Ball Super when she became Future Trunks' love interest in his apocalyptic timeline. In the future timeline, she leads the human resistance against Goku Black alongside Future Trunks, showing courage and leadership far beyond her origins as Pilaf's lackey.

Mai Guides

Grandpa Gohan

The adoptive grandfather who raised Goku in the wilderness after finding him as a Saiyan infant. A master of the Kame style trained directly by Master Roshi, Grandpa Gohan taught Goku martial arts, survival skills, and moral values before being accidentally killed by Goku in his Great Ape form. His spirit later returned for one emotional day during Fortuneteller Baba's tournament, where he fought Goku masked and recognized his grandson's incredible growth before returning to the afterlife with pride.

Grandpa Gohan Guides

Nam

A noble martial artist from a drought-stricken village who entered the 21st Tenkaichi Budokai to win prize money for water for his people. Nam's signature Cross Arm Diving Attack nearly defeated Goku, who only survived due to his tail. After losing, Nam was given water capsules by Master Roshi to bring home. He returned in the 22nd Budokai but was quickly defeated by Tien. Nam represents one of Dragon Ball's earliest themes: martial arts for a purpose beyond fighting.

Nam Guides

Giran

A large winged dinosaur-like monster who participated in the 21st Tenkaichi Budokai. Giran wielded his tail as a weapon and could secrete a sticky gum-like substance to immobilize opponents. After being defeated by Goku in the tournament, Giran returned for the 22nd Budokai where he was eliminated by Chiaotzu's psychic abilities. He later became an unlikely ally, donating energy to Goku's Spirit Bomb against Kid Buu.

Giran Guides

Bacterian

A massive, unhygienic fighter who competed in the 21st Tenkaichi Budokai. Bacterian's primary weapon was his overwhelming body odor and filth, which repulsed opponents and gave him a psychological edge. He nearly defeated Krillin, who was completely overwhelmed by the stench, until Goku reminded Krillin that he had no nose, rendering the odor useless. A comedic one-off character who is nonetheless remembered as one of the most unique early tournament fighters.

Bacterian Guides

Ranfan

A female fighter who used her beauty and seduction as her primary weapon in the 21st Tenkaichi Budokai. Ranfan stripped down to her underwear mid-fight to distract Nam, who was a devout and easily flustered opponent. Though her strategy nearly worked, Nam ultimately overcame his embarrassment and defeated her. Ranfan is notable as one of the earliest female fighters in the Dragon Ball tournament circuit, using unconventional tactics in a world of ki blasts and martial arts.

Ranfan Guides

Pamput

A world-champion boxer and movie star who entered the 22nd Tenkaichi Budokai with immense confidence in his physical prowess. Pamput faced Goku in the preliminaries and was effortlessly defeated when Goku caught his full-power punch with two fingers. The encounter humbled Pamput, who realized there existed a level of martial arts far beyond his championship boxing. He represent the gap between ordinary human athletic achievement and true ki-based combat mastery.

Pamput Guides

Man-Wolf

A literal wolf-man who entered the 22nd Tenkaichi Budokai to compete against the world's best fighters. Man-Wolf faced Jackie Chun (Master Roshi in disguise) and was defeated when Roshi used the Wolf Fang Fist against him, then destroyed the moon to prevent him from transforming into a stronger form. Yes, that actually happened. Man-Wolf is the victim of one of Dragon Ball's most absurd early moments—Roshi casually destroying the moon mid-tournament.

Man-Wolf Guides

Spike the Devil Man

A devil-like warrior who serves Fortuneteller Baba as one of her five fighters. Spike's signature Devilmite Beam is one of the most lethal techniques in the series—it expands and explodes any evil in the target's heart, killing them instantly. Had Goku possessed even a trace of malice, the beam would have killed him. Because Goku was pure-hearted, the attack completely failed, making Spike one of the few villains whose ultimate technique was rendered useless by Goku's innocence.

Spike the Devil Man Guides

Fortuneteller Baba

Master Roshi's older sister and the mystical fortune teller who can locate any lost item for a massive fee—or a battle against her five champions. She rides a crystal ball and serves as a bridge between the living world and the afterlife, employing deceased martial artists as her fighters. Baba has been instrumental in several arcs, from locating the final Dragon Ball in the original series to providing Goku a day pass from the afterlife during the Buu Saga.

Fortuneteller Baba Guides

Korin

The immortal wise cat who lives atop Korin Tower, guarding the sacred Senzu Beans and training warriors who climb the tower. Korin served as Goku's first major trainer after Master Roshi, teaching him to read ki and move faster than sight in just three days. He also trained Master Roshi centuries earlier. Throughout the series, Korin provides Senzu Beans and tactical wisdom to the Z-Fighters, remaining one of Earth's most critical support figures.

Korin Guides

Mr. Popo

The mysterious, dark-skinned, turbaned attendant who serves Earth's Guardian at the Lookout. Mr. Popo is far older and more powerful than he appears, having served multiple generations of Guardians throughout Earth's history. He trained Goku during the three-year gap between King Piccolo's defeat and the 23rd Budokai, teaching foundational principles of ki control and mental clarity. His calm, stoic demeanor and cryptic wisdom make him one of the series' most enigmatic figures.

Mr. Popo Guides

Kami

The former Guardian of Earth and the good half of the Nameless Namekian who split himself to purge evil—creating King Piccolo as his dark counterpart. Kami created Earth's Dragon Balls (originally as Shenron's Dragon Balls) and oversaw the planet for centuries. Though his power is dwarfed by DBZ-era threats, his wisdom and connection to the Dragon Balls made him critical to the story. He eventually refused with Piccolo Jr., restoring the Nameless Namekian's full potential and permanently linking Piccolo's life to the Dragon Balls.

Kami Guides

King Piccolo

The evil half of the Nameless Namekian and the first truly world-threatening villain in Dragon Ball. After being freed by Emperor Pilaf, King Piccolo killed Master Roshi, used the Dragon Balls to restore his youth, and successfully conquered Earth—briefly becoming its ruler. He killed Shenron himself to prevent anyone else from using the Dragon Balls. His reign of terror lasted until Goku finally defeated him with a one-armed Kamehameha punch. Moments before death, King Piccolo spat out an egg containing his reincarnation, Piccolo Jr.

King Piccolo Guides

Tambourine

One of King Piccolo's mutant offspring, created to eliminate martial artists who could potentially use the Evil Containment Wave against his father. Tambourine killed Krillin (temporarily) and systematically hunted down fighters from the World Martial Arts Tournament directory, including many unnamed participants. More powerful than any fighter Goku had faced up to that point, Tambourine was ultimately killed by Goku's vengeful Kamehameha, marking Goku's first kill motivated by pure rage.

Tambourine Guides

Drum

The strongest of King Piccolo's first wave of mutant offspring, created specifically to fight Tien Shinhan after Cymbal and Tambourine had fallen. Drum effortlessly brutalized Tien, who had just defeated one of Piccolo's other warriors, demonstrating the vast power gap between the Demon Clan and Earth's top martial artists. Drum's rampage was cut short when Goku arrived post-Korin training and killed him with a single swift kick to the head, showcasing the results of Goku's training.

Drum Guides

Cymbal

The first of King Piccolo's mutant offspring sent on a mission, Cymbal was tasked with finding the Dragon Balls but encountered Yajirobe instead. The lazy samurai made short work of Cymbal, killing and cooking him for dinner—an encounter that demonstrated Yajirobe's understated strength. Cymbal's death was the first domino that led King Piccolo to personally enter the fray, triggering the King Piccolo Saga's escalation.

Cymbal Guides

Raditz

Goku's older brother and the first Saiyan antagonist to arrive on Earth. Raditz kicked off Dragon Ball Z with the devastating revelation that Goku was a Saiyan sent to destroy Earth. With a power level of 1,500, Raditz was far stronger than Goku and Piccolo combined at the time, requiring them to team up and sacrifice Goku's life to defeat him via Piccolo's Special Beam Cannon. His arrival and transmission to the other Saiyans set the Saiyan Saga in motion, changing Dragon Ball forever.

Raditz Guides

Nappa

The bald, mustachioed Saiyan general who accompanied Vegeta to Earth as his subordinate. Nappa was a brutal, arrogant warrior who casually demolished the Z-Fighters, killing Piccolo, Tien, and Chiaotzu in a single battle. His power level of 4,000 made him unstoppable against anyone but Goku post-King Kai training. After being crippled by Goku's Kaioken assault, Nappa was unceremoniously killed by Vegeta for being a liability—a chilling display of Vegeta's ruthlessness.

Nappa Guides

Zarbon

Frieza's handsome, elegant top lieutenant who served as his right hand during the Namek Saga. Zarbon maintained a beautiful humanoid appearance but could transform into a massive reptilian beast form, vastly increasing his power. He defeated Vegeta in their first encounter, forcing the Saiyan prince into a desperate recovery pod retreat. However, Vegeta returned stronger after the Zenkai boost and brutally killed Zarbon in their rematch, drowning him in the waters of Namek.

Zarbon Guides

Dodoria

A pink, brutish enforcer who served as Frieza's muscle alongside Zarbon. Unlike Zarbon's elegance, Dodoria was pure blunt-force violence, delighting in crushing opponents with his massive frame. He personally participated in the genocide of the Saiyan race on Frieza's orders, killing Bardock's teammates. During the Namek Saga, Dodoria was easily outmaneuvered and killed by Vegeta, who had surpassed him through Zenkai boosts. Before dying, Dodoria revealed to Vegeta that Frieza—not an asteroid—destroyed Planet Vegeta.

Dodoria Guides

Captain Ginyu

The flamboyant purple horned leader of Frieza's elite Ginyu Force, the strongest fighters in his army aside from Frieza himself. Ginyu's body-change technique is one of the most dangerous abilities in the series—he can swap bodies with a target, stealing their power while leaving them in his old weakened form. He successfully stole Goku's body on Namek but was tricked into switching back. Later trapped in a frog's body, Ginyu made a brief return in Dragon Ball Super before being killed by Vegeta.

Captain Ginyu Guides

Jeice

The red-skinned, white-haired Australian-accented member of the Ginyu Force who partnered with Burter for combination attacks. Jeice was the second-in-command of the Ginyu Force and specialized in energy attacks, particularly the Crusher Ball. After Goku effortlessly dismantled the rest of the Ginyu Force, Jeice fled to fetch Captain Ginyu, horrified by Goku's power. He was ultimately killed by Vegeta after Ginyu's defeat, run through by Vegeta's energy blast.

Jeice Guides

Burter

The massive blue alien who claimed to be the fastest being in the universe before meeting Goku. Burter's speed was his pride and primary weapon, allowing him to overwhelm most opponents before they could react. He partnered with Jeice for devastating combination attacks and loved dramatic combat posing. Goku humiliated Burter by moving so fast the blue giant could not even perceive him, then knocked him unconscious in a single blow, shattering Burter's entire self-image as the universe's fastest.

Burter Guides

Recoome

The large, orange-haired humanoid brute of the Ginyu Force whose sheer toughness made him the tank of the squad. Recoome was a sadistic fighter who toyed with his opponents, brutalizing Vegeta, Krillin, and Gohan in devastating fashion during the Namek arc. His durability was extraordinary—he shrugged off Vegeta's strongest attacks as though they were nothing. Goku arrived and knocked Recoome unconscious with a single elbow strike to the gut, shocking everyone present. He was later killed by Vegeta while unconscious.

Recoome Guides

Guldo

The smallest, weakest, and most distinct member of the Ginyu Force—a four-eyed green alien with powerful psychic abilities. Guldo could freeze time for as long as he could hold his breath and use telekinesis to paralyze or attack opponents. His power level of roughly 10,000 was the lowest in the Ginyu Force, but his time-freeze ability made him deadly in specific situations. He was killed by Vegeta while attempting to kill Krillin and Gohan with a sharpened tree trunk during a psychic hold.

Guldo Guides

King Cold

Frieza's father and the former ruler of the Cold Force, a galactic empire spanning countless worlds. King Cold is more physically imposing than Frieza, appearing in his second-form-like state naturally. He accompanied a cyborg-rebuilt Frieza to Earth for revenge against Goku, only to encounter Future Trunks who had arrived from the future. King Cold was completely outmatched by Super Saiyan Trunks, who sliced him in half with his sword before incinerating his remains. His death marked the end of the Cold dynasty.

King Cold Guides

Android 16

A fully mechanical android modeled after Dr. Gero's deceased son, Gevo, who served in the Red Ribbon Army. Unlike Android 17 and 18, Android 16 was designed with a love of nature and a gentle personality, though his primary programming was to kill Goku. He was the physically strongest of the three androids, possessing an internal bomb intended to destroy Goku. 16's death at the hands of Cell—his head crushed underfoot—triggered Gohan's transformation into Super Saiyan 2. His final words to Gohan, urging him to protect the world he loved, became one of the series' most iconic moments.

Android 16 Guides

Android 17

The twin brother of Android 18, formerly a human delinquent before being kidnapped and modified by Dr. Gero. Originally an antagonist who helped Cell absorb him, Android 17 was revived after the Cell Games and reformed, becoming a dedicated park ranger protecting wildlife on a remote island. His return in Dragon Ball Super's Tournament of Power was one of the series' greatest redemption arcs—he was the last man standing from Universe 7, ultimately winning the entire tournament and wishing back all erased universes. His infinite energy reactor and tactical combat make him a formidable fighter.

Android 17 Guides

Android 19

A rotund, pale android with the ability to absorb ki energy through his palms. Android 19 was the first android activated by Dr. Gero in the Android Saga, attacking a city before confronting the Z-Fighters. He infamously drained Goku's energy by gripping him from behind while Goku suffered from the Heart Virus, nearly killing the Saiyan hero. Vegeta arrived and debuted Super Saiyan, systematically dismantling Android 19 with overwhelming power before crushing his head and destroying him completely.

Android 19 Guides

Dr. Gero

The brilliant but vengeful chief scientist of the Red Ribbon Army who transferred his own brain into an Android body, becoming Android 20. Dr. Gero created all numbered androids, including 16, 17, 18, and Cell, dedicating decades to creating the ultimate weapon to kill Goku for destroying the Red Ribbon Army. His own android body featured energy-absorbing palms similar to Android 19. After being overwhelmed by Vegeta's Super Saiyan power, Gero fled back to his lab to activate 17 and 18, but was decapitated and crushed by Android 17 in a moment of poetic irony.

Dr. Gero Guides

Cell Juniors

Miniature versions of Perfect Cell created during the Cell Games to torment the Z-Fighters and force Gohan to unleash his hidden power. Each Cell Junior possessed power roughly on par with Super Saiyan Vegeta and Trunks at the Cell Games, making them far stronger than most of the fighters present. The seven blue child-like bio-androids brutally attacked Goku, Vegeta, Trunks, Krillin, Piccolo, Tien, and Yamcha simultaneously until Gohan transformed into Super Saiyan 2 and obliterated them all with effortless precision.

Cell Juniors Guides

Kid Trunks

The present-timeline son of Vegeta and Bulma, Trunks is a confident, slightly arrogant child who inherited his father's Saiyan pride and his mother's intelligence. He achieved Super Saiyan at an even younger age than Goten, becoming one of the youngest Super Saiyans in history. Trunks' fusion dance with Goten creates Gotenks, one of the most powerful beings in Universe 7. Unlike his future counterpart, Kid Trunks grew up in peace with loving parents, developing a playful but competitive personality.

Kid Trunks Guides

Videl

Daughter of Mr. Satan and wife of Gohan. Initially a headstrong crime-fighting teenager who discovered Great Saiyaman's identity and blackmailed Gohan into teaching her ki control and flight. Videl's determination and courage impressed everyone, quickly mastering the basics of ki flight—a skill that took most fighters years to develop. She became a loving wife to Gohan and mother to Pan, retiring from active combat. In Dragon Ball Super: Super Hero, she trains Pan in martial arts basics while supporting Gohan's return to fighting.

Videl Guides

Mr. Satan (Hercule)

The bombastic, self-promoting World Martial Arts Champion who is beloved by Earth's general population as the world's strongest fighter. Though his actual power is far below the Z-Fighters, Mr. Satan's genuine courage and people skills have saved the world multiple times. He befriended Fat Buu, preventing the monster's rampage, and convinced Earth's population to donate energy for Goku's Spirit Bomb against Kid Buu. His daughter is Videl and his granddaughter is Pan. For all his bluster, Mr. Satan has earned his place as a true hero through heart and circumstance.

Mr. Satan (Hercule) Guides

Dabura

The Demon King of the Demon Realm who served Babidi as his second-in-command during the Buu Saga. Dabura possessed the ability to turn opponents to stone with his saliva and wielded a magical sword in combat. His power rivaled that of Super Perfect Cell, putting him on par with Gohan at the time. Dabura was ultimately killed by Majin Buu when he confronted Babidi, stabbed through the chest and vaporized. In Dragon Ball Daima, his backstory as ruler of the Demon Realm was significantly expanded, revealing a far more complex history.

Dabura Guides

Babidi

The son of Bibidi and the evil wizard who awakened Majin Buu in the modern era. Babidi came to Earth to harvest energy from fighters to resurrect Buu, using his mind-control Majin spell to enslave powerful warriors, most notably Vegeta (who submitted willingly to close the gap with Goku). Operating from a hidden spaceship, Babidi's forces included Dabura, Yakon, and Pui Pui. His arrogance ultimately led to his death when Buu, tired of being ordered around, casually crushed his head and vaporized him.

Babidi Guides

Supreme Kai (Shin)

The current Supreme Kai of Universe 7, responsible for overseeing creation and balance in the universe. Shin is earnest, well-meaning, and perpetually out of his depth when dealing with fighters like Goku and Vegeta. Originally the East Supreme Kai, he became the sole Supreme Kai of Universe 7 after Buu killed his colleagues eons ago. His life-link to Beerus creates an unusual check on the God of Destruction's actions. Though his power is far below the top fighters, his knowledge of divine hierarchy and ancient history makes him a valuable ally.

Supreme Kai (Shin) Guides

Kibito

The loyal, tall attendant to Supreme Kai Shin who serves as his protector and aide. Kibito possesses healing abilities capable of restoring anyone from the brink of death, making him an invaluable support figure during the Buu Saga. His power level far exceeds most mortals but pales in comparison to the Saiyans. Kibito later fused with Shin via Potara earrings to become Kibito Kai, a combined being with enhanced abilities. They later wished to defuse using the Namekian Dragon Balls.

Kibito Guides

Kibito Kai

The Potara fusion of Supreme Kai Shin and his attendant Kibito, created accidentally during the Buu Saga. The fusion permanently combined their appearances and abilities, granting Shin access to Kibito's healing powers. Kibito Kai served as a divine observer and support figure through the Buu Saga and into Dragon Ball Super. They later wished on the Namekian Dragon Balls to defuse back into Shin and Kibito, preferring their original separate forms.

Kibito Kai Guides

Old Kai (Elder Kaioshin)

An ancient Supreme Kai from 15 generations before Shin who was sealed inside the Z-Sword by a powerful enemy long ago. Released when Gohan accidentally broke the sword during training, Old Kai possesses the unique ability to unlock a being's full potential beyond their natural limits through a lengthy ritual. He performed this ritual on Gohan, creating Ultimate Gohan. Old Kai is perpetually lecherous (he charged Goku a photo of Bulma for his services) but invaluable for his ancient wisdom and potential-unlocking ability. He also gave Goku his life via the Potara earrings.

Old Kai (Elder Kaioshin) Guides

Pikkon

A stoic, powerful warrior from the West Quadrant of the Otherworld who competed in the Otherworld Tournament against Goku. Pikkon defeated Frieza and Cell with ease in hell before facing Goku in the finals, where the two fought to a draw. His signature Thunder Flash Attack and Burning Shoot techniques demonstrated power rivaling Super Saiyan 2 Goku. Though technically filler (non-canon to the manga), Pikkon became a fan favorite and briefly assisted Goku against Janemba in Fusion Reborn before appearing as a cameo in Dragon Ball Super.

Pikkon Guides

Grand Kai

The laid-back, elderly Kai who oversees all four quadrant Kais of the Otherworld. Grand Kai organized the Otherworld Tournament where Goku fought Pikkon and other deceased warriors. Though he rarely displays his power, Grand Kai is implied to be immensely strong, training legendary warriors like Olibu for millennia. He has a fondness for rock music and drives a classic American car around the Grand Kai planet. His role is largely relegated to the DBZ filler and movie-verse, but he remains the highest Kai authority below the Supreme Kais.

Grand Kai Guides

King Yemma

The enormous red demon who judges souls arriving in the Otherworld, determining whether they ascend to Heaven or descend to Hell. King Yemma processes countless souls daily from his desk in the Check-In Station, wielding a massive stamp of judgment. Despite his fearsome appearance, he is fair and just, even allowing Goku and friends special dispensations when the fate of Earth is at stake. He also trained under King Kai, running the Serpentine Road faster than Goku ever could.

King Yemma Guides

West Kai

The Kai who oversees the West Quadrant of the universe, a rival to King Kai (North Kai) and master to Pikkon. West Kai is perpetually jealous of King Kai and his talented fighters, often bickering with him during the Otherworld Tournament. Though a god, his combat power is not particularly remarkable by DBZ standards. His role is largely filler/anime exclusive, but he represents the divine bureaucracy of Universe 7's Kai system above the Guardians of each quadrant.

West Kai Guides

South Kai

A diminutive but dedicated Galactic Patrolman who first appeared in the prequel manga Jaco the Galactic Patrolman, which revealed he was sent to Earth to eliminate the Saiyan infant Kakarot before he could grow into a threat. Jaco instead befriended Bulma's older sister Tights and allowed Goku to live. He returned in Dragon Ball Super as an ally, providing reconnaissance, transportation, and comic relief. Despite his small stature and somewhat cowardly nature, Jaco is a competent patrolman with advanced alien technology and genuine heroism when pushed.

Jaco Guides

Merus

An Angel sent to Universe 7's mortal realm as a Galactic Patrolman to gather intelligence on the criminal situation. Merus was the top officer in the Galactic Patrol, possessing speed and power far beyond any mortal—because he was secretly an Angel. When he used his divine powers to fight Moro directly, he violated Angel law and was erased from existence by the Grand Minister. Goku's grief over Merus's sacrifice directly fueled his mastery of Ultra Instinct. Merus was later resurrected as a mortal with no Angel powers, allowed to continue his beloved work as a Galactic Patrolman.

Merus Guides

Granolah

The last surviving Cerealian, a race with evolved sniper vision capable of targeting vital points across vast distances. Granolah was a child when Frieza's forces, disguised as Great Apes, destroyed his homeworld—a genocide secretly instigated by the Heeters to eliminate Cerealian competition. He grew into the greatest bounty hunter in the universe, using the Cerealian Dragon Balls to wish himself into the strongest warrior in Universe 7 at the cost of his remaining lifespan. His arc explores themes of revenge, historical manipulation, and the cycle of vengeance passed down through generations.

Granolah Guides

Gas

The strongest warrior of the Heeter family, a criminal organization that manipulates galactic events from the shadows. Gas was granted his wish by the Cerealian Dragon Balls to become the strongest warrior in the universe after Granolah's wish expired. His power grew so enormous that his body could barely contain it, aging rapidly as his vast power consumed his lifespan. His grudge against Granolah stems from childhood rivalry and his family's manipulation of both Cerealians and Saiyans. Gas represents the danger of granting ultimate power to someone emotionally unstable.

Gas Guides

Moro

A 10-million-year-old goat-like sorcerer who escaped from the Galactic Patrol prison. Moro's primary ability is planetary life-energy absorption—he can drain the life force from entire planets, killing all inhabitants and consuming the planet's energy to increase his power dramatically. He was originally defeated by the Grand Supreme Kai, who sacrificed his own godly power to seal Moro's magic. After escaping, Moro consumed countless planets across Universe 7, eventually absorbing Seven-Three to copy his abilities and later fusing with the Earth itself, forcing Goku to achieve True Ultra Instinct to defeat him.

Moro Guides

Seven-Three

An artificial being with the ability to copy any opponent's abilities and power level by holding them for 30 minutes. Seven-Three stored copied abilities in crystal orbs on his body, amassing a library of techniques from warriors across the galaxy, including Piccolo's and Gohan's. He was captured by the Galactic Patrol but freed by Moro, who later absorbed Seven-Three into his body to gain access to all his copied abilities. Seven-Three's unique power of ability replication made him one of the most potentially dangerous beings in the universe.

Seven-Three Guides

Zuno

A massively obese, extremely knowledgeable being who lives on a distant planet and knows everything there is to know about everything in the universe. Zuno can answer any question asked of him, but the asker must endure his endless tangents, digressions, and precise measurements before getting to the point—a gag that makes obtaining information from him a test of extreme patience. Despite never fighting, Zuno is one of the most important information brokers in the Dragon Ball universe, having been consulted by Bulma, Zamasu, and various other key figures.

Zuno Guides

Tarble

Vegeta's younger brother who was exiled from Planet Vegeta by King Vegeta because he was born gentle and lacked the aggressive nature expected of a Saiyan warrior. Tarble survived on a remote planet with his alien wife Gure, eventually coming to Earth seeking Vegeta's help against the remnants of Frieza's forces led by Abo and Cado. Though lacking his brother's battle instinct, Tarble is the only Saiyan besides Goku to have married for love and found happiness outside the warrior culture. His existence expands Vegeta's family tree beyond the proud prince's shadow.

Tarble Guides

Ox-King

The giant, jovial king of Fire Mountain and Chi-Chi's father. Ox-King was once a fearsome warrior who terrorized the lands around his castle, but after being defeated by Goku and Master Roshi's Kamehameha, he became a gentle giant and loving father. He trained at the Turtle School alongside Grandpa Gohan. Though he rarely fights in later series, Ox-King provides a warm family presence as Gohan and Goten's grandfather figure, often visiting the Son household to dote on his grandsons and great-granddaughter Pan.

Ox-King Guides

Android 8 (Eighter)

A Frankenstein-like android created by the Red Ribbon Army but who, unlike his fellow androids, possessed a gentle soul who hated violence. Android 8 befriended Goku during the Red Ribbon arc, helping him escape Muscle Tower and standing up to General White despite the threat of a built-in bomb that would detonate if he was insubordinate. Goku befriended Eighter, calling him 'Octagon' and standing up for the gentle giant. After the arc, Android 8 lived peacefully in Jingle Village as a protector, one of Goku's earliest and purest friendships.

Android 8 (Eighter) Guides

Tao Pai Pai

The older brother of Master Shen (Crane Hermit) and the world's deadliest assassin during the original Dragon Ball era. Tao Pai Pai was hired by the Red Ribbon Army to eliminate Goku, and he nearly succeeded—throwing a pillar across the continent and riding it to Goku's location, then defeating the boy with his Dodon Ray. Goku's defeat at Tao's hands motivated his climb up Korin Tower for the Senzu Beans and Sacred Water. Tao was later rebuilt as a cyborg, competing in the 23rd Tenkaichi Budokai before being defeated by Tien Shinhan, who had turned against his Crane School roots.

Tao Pai Pai Guides

Yajirobe

A lazy, gluttonous ronin (masterless samurai) who wields a katana and possesses surprising combat strength despite his complete lack of ambition. Yajirobe killed and ate Cymbal (King Piccolo's son), then helped Goku climb Korin Tower and provided crucial support during the King Piccolo arc. His most iconic moment came in the Saiyan Saga when he cut off Vegeta's tail with a single sword strike, reverting the Great Ape to base form and saving Goku's life. Despite rarely fighting, Yajirobe's occasional interventions have been pivotal at critical moments in the series.

Yajirobe Guides

Upa

The young son of Bora, the guardian of Korin Tower's base. Upa's father was killed by Tao Pai Pai during the Red Ribbon Army arc, devastating the boy. Goku swore to avenge Bora and restore him to life, climbing Korin Tower to gain the power needed to defeat Tao and later using the Dragon Balls to resurrect Bora. Upa represents the innocent victims Goku fought to protect in the original Dragon Ball, and his reunion with his resurrected father is one of the series' most emotional early moments.

Upa Guides

Bora

The giant, muscular guardian of the sacred lands below Korin Tower and father to Upa. Bora was a noble warrior who protected the holy ground from Red Ribbon soldiers, fighting with a massive spear. He was killed by Tao Pai Pai's Dodon Ray when the assassin was hired to eliminate Goku. Goku, devastated, climbed Korin Tower, defeated Tao, gathered the Dragon Balls, and revived Bora—a sequence of events that demonstrated the stakes of the pre-Z era and Goku's uncompromising commitment to righting wrongs.

Bora Guides

Commander Red

The diminutive, egomaniacal commander of the Red Ribbon Army who orchestrated the hunt for the Dragon Balls across the globe. Despite being physically unimposing, Commander Red commanded legions of soldiers, advanced weaponry, and ruthless officers. His true motivation for seeking the Dragon Balls was embarrassingly petty—he simply wanted to become taller. When Staff Officer Black discovered this, disgusted by the waste of resources and lives, he executed Red on the spot and assumed command. Commander Red's pathetic ambition remains one of Dragon Ball's most memorable villain defeats.

Commander Red Guides

General Blue

A blonde, blue-eyed, flamboyantly cruel general of the Red Ribbon Army with powerful psychic abilities, including telekinesis and paralysis. General Blue was Commander Red's most effective officer in the Dragon Ball hunt, nearly killing Goku and Krillin multiple times. He is notable for being one of Dragon Ball's earliest and most problematic stereotypes, depicted with stereotypical gay mannerisms. He was eventually defeated in Penguin Village by Arale Norimaki's comedic chaos, then executed by Tao Pai Pai for failure—using his own tongue to kill him in one of DB's darker moments.

General Blue Guides

Staff Officer Black

The tall, stoic second-in-command of the Red Ribbon Army who served Commander Red loyally until he discovered the true reason for the Dragon Ball hunt—Red simply wanted to be taller. Disgusted by the waste of military resources and lives for such a petty goal, Black executed Red and assumed command, piloting the Battle Jacket mech suit to fight Goku himself. Though the mech was powerful, Goku's raw strength prevailed. Black's disgusted execution of his commander and his subsequent defeat at Goku's hands closed out the Red Ribbon Army's reign of terror.

Staff Officer Black Guides

Suno

A kind-hearted young girl from Jingle Village who befriended Goku during the Red Ribbon Army arc. Suno rescued Goku when he was injured and cold, nursing him back to health in her home. She later helped Android 8 (Eighter) find acceptance in the village despite his monstrous appearance. Suno represents the innocent, good-hearted civilians that Goku fought to protect in the original Dragon Ball. Though she only appeared in one arc, her kindness and bravery in standing up to the Red Ribbon Army left an impression on Goku.

Suno Guides

Annin

The mystical guardian of the Furnace of Eight Divinations at the base of the Five Element Mountain in the Otherworld. Annin is an immortal, elderly woman who controls the sacred fire that burns away evil spirits. She appears when Goku must retrieve the Bansho Fan to put out the fire on Fire Mountain (Ox-King's castle). In what became one of Dragon Ball's oddest filler episodes, Goku must navigate the Otherworld's bureaucracy and fight Annin's guardians to obtain the fan. Her role, though small, adds depth to the Otherworld's mythology.

Annin Guides

Piccolo Jr.

The reincarnation of King Piccolo, hatched from the egg his father spat out moments before death, imbued with all of King Piccolo's memories and hatred for Goku. Piccolo Jr. entered the 23rd Tenkaichi Budokai at age 3 (physically an adult due to Namekian growth) specifically to kill Goku and avenge his father. After losing to Goku in the tournament finals, Piccolo Jr. was spared and slowly evolved from vengeful demon to Earth's most steadfast protector. This is the same Piccolo who would later train Gohan and sacrifice himself to save him, but the 'Jr.' designation marks his early, villainous incarnation.

Piccolo Jr. Guides

Shen (Hero)

The human disguise adopted by Kami (Earth's Guardian) to compete in the 23rd Tenkaichi Budokai. Shen was Kami's vessel to enter the tournament and confront Piccolo Jr., the reincarnation of his evil half. Fighting as a seemingly ordinary human, Shen used Kami's divine abilities subtly until his true identity was exposed by Piccolo Jr.'s attack. His plan to seal Piccolo using the Evil Containment Wave (Mafuba) failed when Piccolo reversed the technique, trapping Kami instead. This tournament arc revealed the full scope of the Kami-Piccolo connection.

Shen (Hero) Guides

Meta-Cooler

The resurrected form of Cooler, revived and enhanced by the Big Gete Star—a sentient, planet-sized machine that absorbed Cooler's remains after his defeat. Meta-Cooler's body was now entirely mechanical, allowing him to create infinite duplicates of himself and repair any damage instantly. The Big Gete Star's consumption of New Namek threatened to give Cooler unlimited resources to conquer the universe. Meta-Cooler was ultimately destroyed from within when Goku and Vegeta overloaded the Big Gete Star's core with ki energy, destroying the entire machine planet.
