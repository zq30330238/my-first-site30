Dark Moon Greatsword Guide: Stats, Location, and Best Build

April 9, 2025 · 6 min read

The Dark Moon Greatsword is one of the most iconic weapons in Elden Ring, offering a blend of high intelligence scaling, frost buildup, and a powerful ranged weapon art. This guide covers everything you need to wield it effectively, from acquisition to optimal stat investment.

Where to Find the Dark Moon Greatsword

To obtain the Dark Moon Greatsword, you must complete Ranni the Witch's questline. After defeating the Elden Beast and choosing to become Ranni's consort, or by fully completing her quest and obtaining the Dark Moon Greatsword as a reward. Specifically, after giving Ranni the Fingerslayer Blade and defeating the Baleful Shadow, speak to her at the top of Renna's Rise to receive the sword. Alternatively, if you miss this step, you can find the sword on a body near the Site of Grace in the Cathedral of Manus Celes after Rani's quest is done.

Dark Moon Greatsword Stats and Requirements

The Dark Moon Greatsword requires 16 Strength, 11 Dexterity, and 38 Intelligence to wield one-handed. It scales primarily with Intelligence (B at +10), with D scaling in Strength and Dexterity. It deals 130 physical and 130 magic damage at +10, plus 55 frost buildup per hit. The weapon skill, Moonlight Greatsword, coats the blade in frost and magic damage for 60 seconds, then fires a projectile that deals massive magic damage and frost buildup. The projectile costs 15 FP per shot and scales purely with Intelligence.

Pro Tip: Use the Moonlight Greatsword skill to buff the blade before engaging enemies. The buff lasts 60 seconds and increases damage by 150 flat magic damage on top of the weapon's base. Spam the heavy attack projectile in boss fights to maintain distance.

Best Build for the Dark Moon Greatsword

Focus on Intelligence as your primary stat, reaching at least 60-80 for maximum damage output. Strength and Dexterity should meet only the minimum requirements unless you want to powerstance with another greatsword. Put points into Mind (20-30) to sustain the weapon art's FP cost, and Vigor (40+) for survivability. Use the Lusat's Glintstone Crown for extra Intelligence but lower FP. Talismans: Magic Scorpion Charm (+12% magic damage), Shard of Alexander (+15% weapon skill damage), and Godfrey Icon (+15% charged skill damage). Shield options are limited because you need both hands for the weapon; consider a staff like Carian Regal Scepter in your off-hand for situational spells. Armor can be light or medium; prioritize poise to avoid interruption during heavy attacks.

In combat, use the weapon art buff, then follow up with charged heavy attacks or jump attacks to stack frost quickly. Against enemies immune to frost, rely on the raw magic damage. For bosses like Malenia, the projectile's range lets you punish openings safely. This build excels in PvE and is also viable in PvP with careful spacing.
