Broadway and Theater: Shows You Must See

May 21, 2026 6 min read

Broadway’s Hottest Tickets: The Celeb-Packed Shows You Absolutely Cannot Miss

Darling, the Great White Way is back and shinier than ever – and the stars are out in full force. After a few seasons of quiet, Broadway is roaring with A-list names, record-breaking ticket sales, and enough backstage gossip to fill a season of Palm Royale. Whether you’re a theater queen or just here for the celebrity sightings, this season’s lineup is pure麻辣. From Daniel Radcliffe’s Sondheim triumph to Alicia Keys’s heart-on-her-sleeve musical, here are the shows you must stalk – I mean, see – right now.

“Merrily We Roll Along”: The Sondheim Revival Everyone’s Talking About

If you haven’t heard the screams coming from the Hudson Theatre, you’re living under a rock. The Maria Friedman-directed revival of Stephen Sondheim’s Merrily We Roll Along is the hottest ticket in town, and it’s not just because of the score. The triple threat cast – Daniel Radcliffe, Jonathan Groff, and Lindsay Mendez – has turned this once-difficult show into the most moving, crowd-pleasing experience of the season. Rumors are swirling that the three lead actors have become inseparable offstage, often spotted grabbing late-night dinners at Sardi’s. “The chemistry is insane,” a source told EntertainmentBuzz. “They finish each other’s sentences – and tears – every night.” Even better: Radcliffe has been seen sneaking into the audience during intermission to watch the second act from the mezzanine. The man is obsessed, and so are we.

“Working with Daniel Radcliffe on stage is like playing with a firecracker – you never know when he’ll explode with brilliance!” – Jonathan Groff, on their Merrily partnership

“Hell’s Kitchen”: Alicia Keys’s Broadway Baby (and the Stars Stealing the Show)

Alicia Keys took her autobiographical concept and turned it into a jukebox musical that feels anything but jukey. Hell’s Kitchen, currently at the Shubert Theatre, is a sizzling, soul-baring journey through her teenage years in New York City. The breakout star? Maleah Joi Moon, who plays the Keys stand-in Ali. “She’s a revelation,” raved The New York Times. But the real gossip? Keys herself has been spotted at nearly every performance, sometimes crying in the front row, sometimes sneaking backstage to give notes. “She’s like a stage mother – but with 16 Grammys,” a crew member whispered. Plus, the show’s choreography by Camille A. Brown has everyone talking, and we heard that Beyoncé’s team has been calling for DVD copies. If you want to see a show where the music makes you stand up and the drama makes you gasp, this is it. Oh, and word on the street: Jennifer Hudson was seen attending with Aretha Franklin’s ghost writer. (Okay, that last part is speculation – but wouldn’t that be divine?)

“The Who’s Tommy”: A Rock Opera Reborn

Get ready to deafen your ears – in the best way. The revival of The Who’s Tommy, directed by Des McAnuff (who helmed the original 1993 production), is a sensory overload of guitars, projections, and raw emotion. The star? Ali Louis Bourzgui, a relative newcomer whose jaw-dropping vocals have already earned him comparisons to the young Roger Daltrey. The gossip? Pete Townshend himself has been a frequent presence at rehearsals, and there’s chatter that a certain rock legend (cough, Dave Grohl) might make a surprise cameo on the closing night. The show’s relentless energy has turned its young cast into overnight sensations – and we’re not just talking about the pinball wizard. One insider told us that the actor playing the Acid Queen is dating a famous TikTok dancer, and the drama behind the scenes is almost as electric as the music.

Upcoming Previews: “The Great Gatsby” and “The Outsiders” – Get Your Glitter On!

Hold onto your champagne flutes because the fall season is bringing two literary adaptations that have already sparked bidding wars for tickets. The Great Gatsby musical, starring the dream duo Jeremy Jordan and Eva Noblezada, is set to open at the Broadway Theatre. “The chemistry in rehearsal is off the charts – they’re already making the crew blush,” a source reported. The show features a score by Florence Welch (of Florence + the Machine fame), which means we’re expecting ethereal vocals and enough tragic glamour to fill West Egg. Meanwhile, The Outsiders musical – based on the iconic S.E. Hinton novel and the Coppola film – is shaping up to be the young-blood hit of the season. With a book by Adam Rapp and music by Jamestown Revival, the show has already had an out-of-town tryout in Chicago that left audiences in tears. “The cast is like a boy band meets West Side Story,” said one early reviewer. Expect major TikTok moments and a brand-new soundtrack you’ll be crying to.

Insider Tips for Spotting Celebrities and Snagging Tickets

Now that you know which shows to stalk, here’s how to live your best theater-gossip life. First, always book for mid-week performances – Tuesday and Wednesday are when the biggest names tend to pop in (less paparazzi, more intimacy). Second, if you want to see a star, hit the stage door about 45 minutes after curtain. For Merrily, Daniel Radcliffe often signs for at least 30 minutes, and he’s been known to take selfies with fans dressed as Harry Potter (yes, it’s happened). For Hell’s Kitchen, Alicia Keys has been seen handing out signed CDs to the first 20 people in line. Finally, if you want the best celeb-spotting odds, book a seat at the Sardi’s restaurant before the show – practically every Broadway star dines there. Just don’t be surprised if you end up sharing a restroom mirror with Lin-Manuel Miranda. This is New York, darling – the drama never stops.
