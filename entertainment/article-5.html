Best New TV Series Everyone Is Talking About

May 21, 2026 5 min read

The Show That’s Breaking the Internet: ‘Starlight Secrets’

If you haven’t heard about Starlight Secrets, you’ve probably been living under a rock—or, more likely, you’re just not on TikTok right now. This new drama series from Netflix has taken over every feed, group chat, and red carpet conversation in Hollywood. Starring a dream cast that includes Jenna Ortega, Jacob Elordi, and newcomer Lola Montez, the show follows a group of young influencers on a fictional island where nothing is what it seems—and everyone has a scandal to hide.

“It’s like Euphoria meets The White Lotus with a splash of Gossip Girl,” our insider spills. “But honestly? It’s even messier.” Since its premiere just two weeks ago, Starlight Secrets has already racked up a billion minutes of streaming time. Even the cast is obsessed: reports say Selena Gomez binge-watched the entire season in one night and immediately DM’d Jenna Ortega to beg for spoilers. (Ortega allegedly replied with a single emoji—a peach.)

The buzz is so loud that the show’s hashtag has trended on X (formerly Twitter) for 12 consecutive days. Fans are dissecting every frame, posting fan theories about who the mysterious “Ghost” narrator is, and demanding a second season—which Netflix has already greenlit, by the way. Let’s just say the production team is scrambling to add more security after a paparazzo snuck onto set last week trying to get a shot of Elordi and Montez filming what witnesses describe as a “very heated argument.”

“I’ve never seen a show hook me this fast. The first episode alone has more plot twists than most movies. And the cast? Chefs kiss. I’m literally starting a fan club for Lola Montez.” — Fan tweet with 450K likes

Why Everyone (Including A-Listers) Can’t Stop Watching

It’s not just Gen Z obsessing over Starlight Secrets. A-listers across the board are publicly declaring their love for the series. Kim Kardashian posted a story of herself crying into a bowl of popcorn after episode 4, while Dwayne Johnson was spotted wearing the show’s signature “#SS” hoodie at a charity event. Even former First Lady Michelle Obama tweeted, “I’m invested. Who else thinks Maya is the real villain?” The internet exploded.

Part of the appeal is the show’s uncanny ability to mirror real-life celebrity scandals. The plot revolves around five influencers competing for a $10 million prize while dealing with leaked DMs, secret alliances, and a mysterious figure threatening to expose their darkest secrets. Sound familiar? “It’s basically what happens on every reality show, but with better lighting and wardrobe,” jokes showrunner Ava Reyes. “We wanted to capture that feeling of scrolling through your feed and thinking, ‘Wow, these people are messy. I can’t look away.’”

But the real secret sauce? The chemistry between the leads. Rumors of an off-screen romance between Jenna Ortega and Jacob Elordi have been swirling since they were spotted having dinner at a private club in Los Angeles. Neither has confirmed, but Elordi was recently photographed wearing a necklace with Ortega’s initial. Our sources say they’re “just very close friends”—but let’s be real, Hollywood romances are born from less.

The Cast Drama: Feuds, Romances, and Surprise Cameos

Where there’s smoke, there’s fire—and Starlight Secrets has more behind-the-scenes drama than its own script. The biggest feud? Rumor has it that Lola Montez (who plays the scheming influencer Bianca) and Hailee Steinfeld (who portrays the sweet-talking vlogger Chloe) have been at odds since day one. “Lola showed up to set with a different hairstyle than was approved, and Hailee apparently made a comment about it,” a crew member dished. “They haven’t spoken since except for on-camera scenes.” Steinfeld’s rep has denied any tension, but eagle-eyed fans noticed she unfollowed Montez on Instagram right after the premiere.

On the flip side, there’s a budding bromance between Jacob Elordi and newcomer Mateo Garcia, who plays a mysterious hacker. The two have been posting gym selfies together and were recently caught crashing a fan’s wedding in Vegas (the couple invited them, apparently). “Mateo is just the sweetest guy,” Elordi said in a recent interview. “We have a running joke about who can get the most tattoos during the season.”

And the cameos! Episode 5 featured a blink-and-you’ll-miss-it appearance by Zendaya, playing a waitress who drops a tray of drinks at the exact moment a secret is revealed. Fan theories suggest she’s actually the ghost narrator—but the show’s creators are staying tight-lipped. Also spotted in the background of a party scene: Timothée Chalamet and Lily-Rose Depp, who apparently just happened to be on set that day. Sure, Timmy.

  • Jenna Ortega as Maya, the reality star with a hidden past
  • Jacob Elordi as Ryan, the charming but manipulative player
  • Lola Montez as Bianca, the villain everyone loves to hate
  • Hailee Steinfeld as Chloe, the audience’s moral compass (or is she?)
  • Mateo Garcia as Ghost, the hacker who knows everything

What the Critics Are Saying (Spoiler: It’s Glowing)

The reviews are in, and they’re basically a love letter. Variety called it “the most addictive show of the year, packed with performances that will make you scream,” while Rolling Stone hailed Montez as “the breakout star we didn’t know we needed.” On Rotten Tomatoes, the series holds a 94% critics score and an even higher 97% audience rating—a rare feat for a binge-able drama.

“Starlight Secrets doesn’t just entertain; it satirizes the influencer culture we all pretend to hate but secretly consume,” writes critic Sandra Hayes. “And the cast is so charismatic that you’ll root for characters who are objectively terrible people.” Indeed, the show has already sparked countless think pieces about social media, privacy, and the price of fame. But who cares about that when you can just enjoy the mess, right?

One particularly viral moment came in episode 3, when Maya (Ortega) delivers a monologue about betrayal while holding a glass of rosé that slowly turns to water (symbolism, honey!). That scene alone has been remixed into 10,000 TikToks. Even the show’s soundtrack, featuring tracks from Olivia Rodrigo and Bad Bunny, is climbing the charts. “We wanted to make a show that feels like a cultural event,” says producer Jason Blum. “Mission accomplished.”

Where to Watch and What’s Next for Season 2

You can stream all eight episodes of Starlight Secrets exclusively on Netflix right now. The good news: there’s no weekly wait—it’s a full drop, perfect for your weekend plans. The bad news: you’ll probably finish it in a single day, and then you’ll be left craving more like the rest of us.

But don’t worry—Season 2 is already in pre-production, with a target release date of early next year. Showrunner Ava Reyes teased that “things are about to get even more twisted. We’re introducing a new character who will shake up the entire dynamic, and someone we love won’t make it past the first two episodes.” Who will be the first to go? Fans are already placing bets, and the cast is having a blast feeding the speculation. Jacob Elordi posted a blurry photo of a script page with the word “DEATH” circled—though he quickly deleted it.

Netflix is also reportedly considering a reality competition spin-off called Starlight: Under the Influencer, where real influencers try to survive an island with no WiFi. If it happens, you know we’ll be watching.

“I can’t believe they killed off my favorite character in episode 6. I literally screamed. But also, the plot twist? Genius. I’m never trusting a white couch again.” — A celebrity stylist (who wishes to remain anonymous) after a private screening

Final Verdict: Is It Worth the Hype?

In a word: absolutely. Starlight Secrets delivers on every front—gorgeous cast, addictive storytelling, and just enough real-world gossip to keep you refreshing your feeds. Whether you’re in it for the fashion (the “Bianca Green” manicure is already a trend on Pinterest), the mysteries, or the jaw-dropping twists, this show is a must-watch. And with the celebrity-fueled drama spilling off-screen, it’s the kind of entertainment that keeps on giving.

So grab your popcorn, put your phone on Do Not Disturb, and prepare to lose a few hours. Just don’t blame us when you end up staying awake until 3 a.m. texting your friends about that final scene. Oh, and one more thing: if you haven’t watched yet, stay off social media. Spoilers are everywhere. Trust us—you don’t want to know what happens to the dog. (Just kidding. Or are we?)
