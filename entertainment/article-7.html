Upcoming Music Albums to Get Excited About

May 21, 2026 6 min read

Get Ready to Bop: The Most Anticipated Albums Dropping This Year (And the Scandalous Rumors We’re Living For)

Buckle up, stans – 2025 is shaping up to be a chaotic, iconic, and downright juicy year for music. From surprise drops that will break the internet to simmering feuds threatening to spill into lyrics, the upcoming album slate is already giving drama, emotion, and banger after banger. We’ve got the inside scoop on who’s recording, who’s feuding, and which secret collab might just send Twitter into meltdown. Grab your headphones and your best gossip voice, because we’re breaking down the albums you absolutely cannot miss.

Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour Aftermath – A Double Album or a Love Letter to Travis?

Let’s be real – Taylor Swift never stops. After wrapping the highest-grossing tour in history, sources close to the pop queen say she’s already back in the studio. But here’s the tea: insiders whisper this next project might not be a typical Taylor’s Version re-record. Instead, Swift is reportedly crafting an entirely new double album, with one side dedicated to her whirlwind romance with NFL star Travis Kelce and the other… to her exes? “She’s writing like she’s possessed,” a studio insider told us. “One track literally samples a voicemail from a certain British ex-boyfriend – and it’s not pretty.”

Fans are already decoding Easter eggs: a Polaroid posted from Electric Lady Studios showed a piano with a single rose and a Chiefs beanie. Coincidence? We think not. Expect vulnerable ballads, stadium-ready anthems, and maybe a few subtle disses aimed at Scooter Braun. When asked for comment, Taylor’s team simply replied with a music note emoji. Cryptic? Always. Exciting? Absolutely.

Fun fact: Taylor’s 2020 album folklore was written and recorded in complete secrecy during the pandemic – and she only told her closest collaborator, Aaron Dessner, 48 hours before the drop. So don’t be shocked if this new project appears on streaming with zero warning.

Beyoncé’s Renaissance Follow-Up: Country, Céline Dion, and a Feud with the Chicks?

Queen Bey teased a “three-act” project, and after the house and disco euphoria of Renaissance (Act I), Act II is rumored to be a country-and-americana explosion. If the horse-riding visuals and the Stetson hats at her Dubai gig were any clue, Beyoncé is about to take over Nashville. But here’s the gossip: a source at Parkwood Entertainment claimed she’s collaborating with Céline Dion on a power ballad, while also allegedly sampling a track by The Chicks – whom she once snubbed at the CMAs. “She’s reclaiming country music’s roots and calling out the gatekeepers,” the source said. And in true Beyoncé fashion, the rollout is pure chaos. A teaser video showed a ranch on fire, a black cowboy hat, and the words “Cowboy Carter” – presumably her alter ego for the era.

What we know: the album is mastered and ready, but Beyoncé is holding it until the right marketing moment. “She wants to drop it when the world least expects it,” says a former Sony executive. And with a rumored feature from Dolly Parton? Sugar, we’re already shaking our wigs.

  • Rumored Track Titles: “Leather & Lace (feat. Céline Dion)”, “Daddy’s Lessons (Revisited)”, “Beyoncé & Willie Nelson? (TBD)”
  • Confirmed Producers: Raphael Saadiq, No I.D., and potentially Jack Antonoff (gasp)
  • Release Window: Spring 2025, but don’t hold your breath

Harry Styles’ Genre-Bending Return: Indie Rock, Samba, and a Breakup Album?

Harry Styles has been notoriously quiet since his Love on Tour finale – and we all know what that means: new music is coming. But whispers suggest this album is a complete departure from Harry’s House. “He’s been living in Rio de Janeiro for three months, learning Portuguese and jamming with local samba bands,” a close friend dished to EntertainmentBuzz. “The sound is gritty, raw, and surprisingly angry.” Rumors of a split from his current flame have also fueled speculation that this will be his angsty, heartbreak album. Think Fine Line but with more distortion and less feather boas.

One thing that has insiders buzzing: Harry allegedly recorded a secret diss track directed at a certain “prince of pop” who shall remain nameless (but rhymes with “Shawn Mendes”?). The lyrics reportedly include the line “You can keep your blond hair and your fake humility.” Ouch. Meanwhile, fans spotted him at London’s iconic Abbey Road Studios with a full orchestra. Could we be getting strings, samba, and screaming guitars? Yes, please.

  • Working Title: Late Night Fires (subject to change)
  • Potential Features: Anderson .Paak, Rosalía, and – wait for it – a surprise appearance by Elton John
  • Release Date Prediction: Late summer 2025, just before his 32nd birthday

K-Pop Takeover: BTS Solo Albums, BLACKPINK Solo Debuts, and a New Girl Group Feud

The K-pop world is about to get a whole lot louder. BTS members are continuing their solo journeys – Jungkook’s full album after his smash hit “Standing Next to You” is expected to drop any day, with a rumored collab with Justin Bieber that would break streaming records. Meanwhile, J-Hope is back from military service and allegedly working on a “dark, autobiographical” record that addresses his mental health battles. Over at YG Entertainment, BLACKPINK’s Jennie and Lisa are each preparing debut solo albums after years of singles. “Jennie’s album is confessional and rock-influenced, while Lisa’s is pure hip-hop bravado,” a YG insider leaked. But the drama doesn’t stop there: a new girl group from HYBE, tentatively called VIVID, is already embroiled in a plagiarism scandal before releasing a single note – fans are accusing them of copying BLACKPINK’s entire aesthetic. The shade is real, and the albums haven’t even dropped.

Fun fact: BTS’s V recorded his entire debut album Layover in a single week while suffering from a cold, and the raw, breathy vocals became a signature sound. Expect similar spontaneity from his upcoming solo project, which is rumored to feature a duet with his famous dog, Yeontan.

Comebacks We Didn’t See Coming: Miley Cyrus, Rihanna, and a Secret Duet Album

Just when you thought you were safe, the industry’s biggest ghosts are rising from the dead. Miley Cyrus, fresh off her Grammy win for “Flowers,” is teasing a “psychedelic country” album produced by her father and Brandi Carlile. “She’s been spending time on her Tennessee farm, writing about her divorce and newfound freedom,” a source says. “Think Dolly Parton meets Tame Impala.” But the real jaw-dropper? Rihanna – yes, the Rihanna – has apparently been secretly recording in Barbados with Pharrell Williams. After years of Fenty taking over her life, Navy is finally getting their wish. “R9 is real,” a UMG insider confirms. “It’s reggae, dancehall, and a dash of pop – pure Rihanna. And there’s a second album coming in 2026.” Wait, a double Rihanna era?! Meanwhile, rumors of a secret duet album between Lady Gaga and Bruno Mars have popped up after they were spotted leaving a studio together in matching leather jackets. Could we be looking at a full Motown-inspired project? The industry is buzzing.

  • Miley Cyrus: Tennessee Honey (working title) – Summer 2025
  • Rihanna: Untitled (R9) – possibly Halloween 2025 (she loves a surprise)
  • Lady Gaga & Bruno Mars: Project name “Mars & Venus” – early fall 2025

One More Bite: The Album That Might Spark a Chart War

If all these releases drop in the same window – and they might – we’re looking at the most epic chart battle since Oasis vs. Blur. Imagine Taylor Swift, Beyoncé, Harry Styles, and Rihanna all vying for the #1 spot in the same month. Record labels are already adjusting release dates, hoping to avoid a bloodbath. But let’s be real: we, the fans, win. This year’s music lineup is so stacked that even the Grammys are reportedly changing voting rules to accommodate the onslaught. So pre-save, pre-order, and prepare your group chats. The albums are coming – and they’re bringing all the tea, tears, and TikTok dances. Pop music has never been this messy, and we are so here for it.

Which upcoming album are you most hyped for? Drop your takes on EntertainmentBuzz socials – and stay tuned for more leaks, tracklist reveals, and behind-the-scenes drama. 2025 is about to be loud, chaotic, and absolutely unforgettable.
