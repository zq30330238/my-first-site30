Flint-Knock Pistol Guide: Master Close-Quarters Chaos

April 2, 2025 · 5 min read

The Flint-Knock Pistol is the weapon that rewards aggression and creativity. You don't pick it for consistency. You pick it for those moments where you need to delete a box, knock an opponent off high ground, or escape a bad fight. This guide breaks down exactly how to use the Flint-Knock's unique mechanics to control fights and catch enemies off guard.

How the Flint-Knock Works

The Flint-Knock is a pistol that fires a single slug with high damage and massive knockback. It has a tight spread but a long reload. Each shot pushes you backward if you are in the air, or keeps you grounded if you are standing on a surface. This creates two distinct uses: offense and mobility.

At close range, a body shot deals 86 damage to a player with full shield, meaning it won't one-shot a 200-HP target. A headshot hits for 172, leaving most players with just 28 HP. That's enough to finish with a pickaxe or an SMG spray. The knockback on enemies sends them flying, often breaking their builds or knocking them off an edge.

When you shoot while jumping, you get a huge backward impulse. This lets you rocket-jump without a rocket launcher. You can use it to retreat instantly, scale a wall by shooting downward, or surprise an enemy by appearing above them after a well-timed shot.

Shoot the floor just as you jump to launch yourself up a ramp or onto a roof. It acts as a free Shockwave Hammer charge, but you keep your weapon slot. Practice in Creative before taking it into a match.

Best Loadout and Playstyle

The Flint-Knock works best as a secondary weapon. Pair it with an automatic weapon like a Striker AR or a Pulse Rifle for mid-range pressure. I carry an SMG or a Combat Shotgun for finishing kills after the initial Flint-Knock shot. Always keep a shotgun or a spray weapon ready to clean up what the Flint-Knock starts.

Your playstyle should revolve around creating spacing. Use the Flint-Knock to knock opponents back when they push your box. Then swap to your AR and pepper them while they scramble to rebuild. Alternatively, knock them off high ground and make them waste mats catching themselves.

You can also use it for rotations. In endgame, if you get caught in a bad storm position, shoot the ground to launch yourself toward the safe zone. This is risky because you might get beamed, but it can save your match when you have no launch pad.

When to Use It (and When to Swap)

Pick up the Flint-Knock early game because it one-shots a player with no shield. It also works well against AIs. Mid-game, keep it if you have good shotgun aim and want mobility. Swap it out in endgame if you don't have a consistent close-range weapon. The Flint-Knock can't replace a pump shotgun for tight box fights.

Practice aiming the shot while moving. The weapon has a slight bloom, so wait for the reticle to settle. Always try to get headshots. A body shot might not scare a good player, but a headshot makes them panic. Use that panic to take wall control or edit through their box.

The Flint-Knock Pistol is not the meta pick for every game, but it adds an unpredictable element that can turn a losing fight into a highlight clip. Learn the knockback angles, practice the jump-shot disengage, and you will find moments where no other weapon works as well.
