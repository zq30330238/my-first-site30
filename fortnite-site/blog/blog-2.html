Mammoth Pistol Guide: Dominate Mid-Range in Fortnite

April 7, 2025 · 5 min read

The Mammoth Pistol has carved a niche as a high-skill, high-reward weapon in Fortnite. Available as a rare (blue) to legendary (gold) drop, this hand cannon packs a punch with its single-shot, high-damage profile. It excels at mid-range engagements where its accuracy and stopping power can turn the tide of a fight. Unlike spray weapons, the Mammoth demands precision but rewards you with the ability to break structures and eliminate opponents in just a few shots.

Mammoth Pistol Stats and Performance

At legendary rarity, the Mammoth Pistol deals 73 damage to the body and 146 to the head. That means a two-tap headshot eliminates a full-health opponent. It has a fire rate of 0.65 (roughly one shot per 1.5 seconds) and a magazine size of 6. Reload time is 2.2 seconds. The weapon has no damage falloff, making it effective at surprisingly long ranges, though bullet travel time becomes noticeable beyond 80 meters. The Mammoth also deals extra structure damage--120 per shot--so you can quickly break through wood walls or peek holes.

This weapon shares similarities with the Deagle (Hand Cannon) from previous seasons but has a slightly slower fire rate and higher damage. It uses Light Bullets, making ammo management easier. However, the low fire rate means you cannot afford to miss. Every shot counts.

Pro Tip: Use the Mammoth Pistol to finish opponents after dealing initial damage with a spray weapon. The high single-shot damage lets you confirm kills quickly without exposing yourself to return fire.

Best Strategies for the Mammoth Pistol

To maximize the Mammoth Pistol, focus on positioning and crosshair placement. Peek from behind cover, line up your shot, and fire as you expose yourself. The weapon's accuracy is excellent while crouching or standing still, but moving reduces hip-fire spread. Always aim down sights for optimum precision. Pair the Mammoth with a close-range weapon like a SMG or shotgun to handle opponents who rush you during the long reload cycle.

In build fights, use the Mammoth to break enemy walls before switching to an AR or SMG for chip damage. The structure damage is strong enough to force opponents into a box fight where you can predict their edits. Another effective tactic is to use the Mammoth for fast peeks: jump out of a box, fire one shot, then immediately build back. This minimizes exposure while still applying pressure.

For zero build modes, stay at medium range and use natural cover. The Mammoth is lethal against unshielded players, especially if you can catch them rotating. Practice flick shots on moving targets to improve your consistency. Remember that you can pre-fire corners or doorways to catch enemies off guard.

When to Swap or Drop the Mammoth

The Mammoth Pistol is not a weapon for every loadout. It struggles in chaotic close-range fights where spamming shots is more effective. If you find yourself in tight spaces frequently, trade the Mammoth for a shotgun or SMG. Conversely, if you have a spray weapon for close range and a sniper for long range, the Mammoth fills a unique mid-range role that no other weapon covers as efficiently.

Drop the Mammoth when your aim is off or you're taking too long to line up shots. It's also a trap to hold it as your only weapon--always carry a secondary for different ranges. In team modes, the Mammoth works well for a designated 'peeker' who applies pressure while teammates flush out enemies.
