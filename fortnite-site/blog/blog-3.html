Ranger Shotgun Guide: Dominate Mid-Range in Fortnite

March 26, 2025 · 6 min read

Ranger Shotgun Stats and Overview

The Ranger Shotgun is a mid-range powerhouse in Fortnite Chapter 6. It fires a tight spread of pellets with high damage per shot, making it ideal for players who prefer precision over spam. Available in Common through Legendary rarities, it deals 73-80 body damage and 146-160 headshot damage. Its magazine holds 5 shells, and it has a moderate fire rate of 0.7 seconds between shots. The weapon's standout feature is its accuracy--pellet spread is nearly 30% tighter than a Tactical Shotgun at the same range. This makes it a threat at ranges where other shotguns start missing. However, its slow fire rate means you cannot miss. Reload time is 4.5 seconds for a full magazine, so plan your engagements.

Best Loadout Combos

Pair the Ranger Shotgun with a high-DPS SMG like the Twin Mag SMG to cover close-range weakness. Alternatively, use a Long Rifle (e.g., Striker Burst or Heavy Sniper) for long-range poke. For maximum efficiency, carry a Shockwave Launcher to create distance when enemies close in. Build combos: Use the Ranger as your opening shot, then switch to an SMG to finish. Against boxed opponents, phase through walls with a pickaxe and land one clean Ranger shot. The Ranger also works well with Bunkers--place a Bunker, take a shot, then reset. Avoid using it with slow-reloading ARs that leave you vulnerable. A mobility item like the Kinetic Blade helps reposition for optimal mid-range angles.

Positioning and Aiming Tips

The Ranger Shotgun excels in open fields and elevated positions. Always aim for the upper chest or neck--pellet spread still hits the head for max damage even if slightly off. Never aim down sights at close range; hipfire is tight enough. Practice flick shots in Creative mode to build muscle memory. When fighting an opponent with a lower-rate weapon, use right-hand peeking to expose only your weapon. Build a wall, edit a window, shoot, then reset. The Ranger's tight spread means you can shoot through small edits like window bars or half-wall ramps.

Pro tip: Aim for the upper chest and head at mid-range to maximize damage while maintaining pellet spread. A Legendary Ranger headshot deals 160 damage, enough to break full shield.
Finally, always carry a heal item--the Ranger's slow fire rate means you'll take chip damage during reload. Use mini-shields or splash to stay in the fight.
