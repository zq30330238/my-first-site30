Species: Human · Stand: None (The Passion) · Affiliation: Joestar Family · First Appearance: Chapter 1 (1987)

Overview

Jonathan Joestar is the original JoJo and the protagonist of Phantom Blood. A gentleman in the truest sense, Jonathan embodies honor, courage, and compassion. His rivalry with his adoptive brother Dio Brando sets the Joestar-DIO conflict in motion. Jonathan's mastery of Hamon and his unwavering sense of justice define the heroic lineage that follows him. Though his life is tragically short, his legacy echoes across generations.

Appearance

Jonathan is tall and powerfully built with dark hair and an honest, open face. He typically wears formal Victorian-era clothing, including suits and top hats befitting his aristocratic upbringing. His muscular physique reflects his dedication to martial training and Hamon practice. Jonathan's design is more classically heroic compared to his descendants, emphasizing his role as a straightforward, honorable fighter.

Personality

Jonathan is the archetypal gentleman, possessing unwavering moral principles and deep compassion for others. He believes in fighting fair, protecting the innocent, and standing up against injustice. Despite Dio's relentless cruelty toward him, Jonathan repeatedly tries to see the good in his adoptive brother. His courage is absolute, and he faces impossible odds without hesitation. Jonathan's nobility inspires deep loyalty in his friends allies, including Speedwagon and Zeppeli.

Abilities

Jonathan learns Hamon from Will A. Zeppeli and becomes a formidable warrior capable of using Hamon through various body parts and objects. His Hamon techniques include the powerful Overdrive attacks that can destroy vampires. Jonathan also possesses great physical strength and swordsmanship skills. He is a fast learner who quickly masters complex techniques when motivated. His final act uses Hamon to destroy Dio's undead body while sacrificing himself.

Trivia

Jonathan's name literally means 'God has given' in Hebrew, fitting his noble character. His Great Dane, Danny, was his childhood companion. Jonathan is the shortest-lived JoJo protagonist, dying at age 20. His body is stolen by Dio, leading to the events of Stardust Crusaders. Araki designed Jonathan as a direct contrast to Dio, pure good versus pure evil.
