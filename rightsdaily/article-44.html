The 5 Best Online Will-Making Services for 2026 - Compared

May 26, 2026 8 min read

Why You Need an Online Will in 2026

Creating a last will and testament is one of the most important legal steps you can take to protect your assets and your loved ones. Yet according to a 2025 Caring.com survey, nearly 60% of American adults still do not have a will. Without one, state intestacy laws determine who inherits your property--often not aligning with your wishes. The good news: online will-making services have made estate planning more accessible, affordable, and faster than ever. In this comparison, we rank the five top services based on cost, legal accuracy, ease of use, and customer support.

According to a 2025 survey by Caring.com, nearly 60% of American adults still do not have a will. Online will services can help close that gap for under $200.

We evaluated each service by creating sample wills for a married person with children, a single person with no heirs, and a pet owner. We weighed factors like state-specific compliance, attorney review options, and added features such as living wills and trusts. Below are our rankings, from fifth place to the best overall service for most Americans.

5. US Legal Forms - Affordable but Bare Bones

US Legal Forms offers a pay-per-document model starting at $29.95 for a basic will. The platform provides state-specific templates that comply with local laws, but the process is entirely DIY--no step-by-step interview, no attorney review, and no digital storage. You fill out PDF forms manually, which increases the risk of errors.

Pros: Lowest price point; state-specific templates; no subscription required. Cons: No guided questionnaire; limited support; no updated legal counsel; no integration with living trusts or health care directives. Best for: Budget-conscious individuals with very simple estates who are comfortable reading legal language.

4. Nolo's WillMaker & Trust - Best for DIY Legal Enthusiasts

Nolo has been a trusted name in DIY legal publishing since the 1970s. Their WillMaker & Trust software costs a flat $49 and includes a comprehensive interview process that covers wills, living trusts, powers of attorney, and health care directives. You download and install the software on your computer.

Pros: Excellent educational content; covers multiple documents; no recurring fees; lifetime updates for the version you buy. Cons: No cloud storage; no attorney review; the interface feels dated; no mobile app. Best for: Tech-savvy users who want a one-time purchase and enjoy reading legal explanations.

3. Rocket Lawyer - Great for Ongoing Legal Needs

Rocket Lawyer operates on a subscription model: $39.99 per month (or $49.99 without a free trial) gives you access to unlimited legal documents, including wills. Non-members can pay $99 per document. The will creation process is guided by an intuitive interview, and every document comes with a free 30-minute attorney consultation.

Pros: Attorney consultation included; unlimited documents for subscribers; easy-to-use interface; mobile app available. Cons: Monthly subscription adds up if you only need one will; the free consultation is limited to basic questions; some users report upsells for add-ons. Best for: Individuals or families who anticipate needing multiple legal documents--such as a will, power of attorney, and rental lease.

2. LegalZoom - The Trusted Name with Comprehensive Options

LegalZoom is one of the most recognized online legal service providers, having helped over 2 million families create wills. Their Estate Plan Bundle starts at $149 for a basic will, with a living will and health care power of attorney included. You can add a living trust, attorney review, and faster shipping for extra fees. The guided interview is thorough and state-specific.

Pros: Highly reputable; includes living will and health care proxy; optional attorney review ($49); excellent customer support; free document storage for the first year. Cons: Costs can quickly exceed $300 with add-ons; no subscription option; cross-state asset planning may require separate trust documents. Best for: Users who want a trusted brand and are willing to pay a moderate premium for peace of mind and optional legal advice.

1. Trust & Will - Best Overall for Simplicity and Value

Trust & Will earns the top spot for its modern, user-friendly platform and transparent pricing. Their Will Plan starts at $159 for a married couple or $89 for an individual, and it includes a living will, guardianship nominations, and digital asset management. The interview process takes about 20 minutes and adjusts based on your state. Trust & Will also offers a full trust-based plan for asset protection and probate avoidance.

Pros: Clear, simple interface; affordable flat fees; includes pet trusts and digital asset clauses; all documents are stored securely online; excellent customer service via chat and email. Cons: No attorney review included (available as a $99 add-on); not ideal for complex estates with multiple properties or business ownership; limited to wills and trusts (no powers of attorney unless you buy a bundle). Best for: The majority of Americans with straightforward to moderately complex estates who want a fast, affordable, and legally sound will.

How to Choose the Right Online Will Service for Your Situation

Before purchasing, consider three factors: the complexity of your estate, your budget, and whether you need future document updates. If you have minor children, a pet, or own a home, Trust & Will or LegalZoom will cover all bases. If you're on a tight budget and comfortable with legal text, Nolo's software works great. If you anticipate needing multiple legal documents beyond a will, Rocket Lawyer's subscription offers the best value. Finally, always check that the service you choose supports your state's specific will formalities--most of these services do, but US Legal Forms requires you to manually verify.

Verdict: Trust & Will Edges Out the Competition

After testing all five services, Trust & Will offers the best combination of price, ease of use, and legal completeness. It's not the cheapest (US Legal Forms is cheaper) and not the most feature-rich (LegalZoom offers more add-ons), but for the vast majority of people, a $159 couple's will that covers guardianship, living will, and digital assets is hard to beat. Nolo remains the best DIY option for those who want maximum control, and Rocket Lawyer is ideal for ongoing legal needs. Whatever you choose, do not delay--drafting a will now ensures your legal rights and your family's future are protected.
