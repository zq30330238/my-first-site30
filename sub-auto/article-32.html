Top 5 Electric SUVs Under $40,000 in 2026: Tested and Reviewed

May 23, 2026 6 min read

Hyundai Ioniq 5: The Gold Standard for Value

The Hyundai Ioniq 5 continues to dominate the affordable EV segment in 2026, thanks to its retro-futuristic design, spacious interior, and class-leading 303 miles of range. Base models start at $37,995, making it one of the few long-range EVs under $40,000. During our real-world testing, we achieved 288 miles on a single charge in mixed driving conditions--impressively close to the EPA estimate.

The Ioniq 5's 800-volt architecture allows for ultra-fast charging from 10% to 80% in just 18 minutes on a 350 kW station. That's a game-changer for road trips. The interior feels premium with recycled materials and dual 12.3-inch screens. Rear seat legroom is best-in-class, and the flat floor adds to passenger comfort.

According to Edmunds, the Ioniq 5 holds its value better than any other EV under $50,000, with a projected 5-year residual of 58%.

Chevrolet Equinox EV: The American Contender

Chevrolet has finally delivered a truly affordable compact SUV with the 2026 Equinox EV. Starting at $35,995, it undercuts many rivals while offering up to 319 miles of range on the single front-motor version. Our testers found the ride quality to be smooth and quiet, though the base infotainment system lacks wireless Apple CarPlay--a noticeable omission.

The Equinox EV comes standard with Chevy Safety Assist, including automatic emergency braking and lane-keeping assist. The dual-motor AWD version adds $4,000 but brings all-weather confidence. However, the interior materials feel a notch below Hyundai and Kia in this price bracket. Still, for the range and price, it's hard to beat.

Kia Niro EV: Practicality Meets Efficiency

Kia's 2026 Niro EV sees a significant refresh, with improved efficiency and a new 68.0 kWh battery delivering 270 miles of range. Starting at $34,890, it's one of the cheapest EVs with a true SUV shape. The Niro's cargo space measures 25.7 cubic feet with the rear seats up--enough for four suitcases and a stroller.

Charging speeds max out at 100 kW, which is slower than the Ioniq 5 and Equinox, but the Niro EV compensates with excellent energy efficiency--we averaged 3.7 mi/kWh in city driving. The cabin features eco-friendly materials like recycled plastic and vegan leather. The 10.25-inch touchscreen is responsive, and the physical climate controls are a welcome relief in an era of touch-only interfaces.

Volkswagen ID.4: European Comfort at a Bargain

Volkswagen's updated 2026 ID.4 now comes with a 82.0 kWh battery and 291 miles of range. The starting price of $36,995 includes a 12.9-inch display and gesture controls--though the touch sliders remain mildly annoying. On the road, the ID.4 shines with a smooth, quiet ride and composed handling. It's not the quickest (7.0 seconds 0-60), but everyday driving feels refined.

The ID.4's strength lies in comfort and interior space. The front seats offer 14-way adjustment, and the back seat is spacious enough for three adults. Volkswagen offers 3 years of free fast charging at Electrify America stations, which can save new owners over $1,000 in the first year. However, the infotainment system can lag occasionally--a known issue VW hasn't fully resolved.

Ford Mustang Mach-E Select: Style with Substance

The lowest-trim Ford Mustang Mach-E starts at $38,990, making it the priciest in this group but offering undeniable curb appeal. The 2026 model year brings a 72.0 kWh battery and 245 miles of range--less than competitors, but real-world testing matched the EPA numbers. The Select trim includes the 15.5-inch vertical screen, wireless Apple CarPlay, and Ford's BlueCruise hands-free highway assist for 90 days.

Handling is sporty with precise steering, and the Mach-E accelerates from 0-60 in 5.1 seconds--the quickest here. Cargo space is 29.7 cubic feet with the rear seats up. Build quality has improved since initial launch, but some plastic trim pieces feel cheap for the price. Overall, the Mach-E scores points for driving dynamics and technology, even if its range trails the competition.
