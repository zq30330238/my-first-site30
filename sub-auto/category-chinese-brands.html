Top 20 Chinese Auto Brands Going Global in 2026

Chinese automakers are no longer just domestic players — they're rapidly expanding into Europe, Southeast Asia, Latin America, and beyond. Here are the 20 brands leading China's automotive revolution.

  1. BYD — World's largest NEV maker, dominating EV sales globally with Seagull, Dolphin, Seal, Han, Atto 3, Yangwang U8
  2. Geely — Owns Volvo/Polestar/Lotus, won TCR World Tour, making global waves with Zeekr premium EVs
  3. NIO — Premium EV brand with battery-swap tech, expanding to Europe with ET7, ET5, EL7
  4. XPeng — 800V platform leader with XNGP autonomous driving, G6 and G9 competing globally
  5. Li Auto — Extended-range EV specialist, best-selling premium SUVs in China with L series
  6. Great Wall Motor (GWM) — Haval/Ora/Tank brands, strong in Australia, Southeast Asia, and South Africa
  7. SAIC Motor — MG brand's European success story, Maxus commercial EVs gaining traction
  8. Chery — China's largest exporter by volume, popular in Russia, Brazil, and Middle East
  9. Changan — Deepal and Avatr sub-brands pushing into EV space, strong in Middle East
  10. GAC Motor — Aion EV brand among top-sellers, Trumpchi expanding to Middle East and SE Asia
  11. Dongfeng — Voyah premium EVs entering Europe, strong commercial vehicle exports
  12. FAW — Hongqi luxury brand targeting premium European segments with E-HS9 electric SUV
  13. BAIC — Arcfox EV sub-brand with Huawei inside, strong in South America
  14. JAC Motors — Strong EV commercial vehicle exports, partnering with Volkswagen
  15. Leapmotor — Cost-competitive EVs with Stellantis backing for European distribution
  16. Neta (Hozon) — Affordable EVs targeting Southeast Asia and emerging markets
  17. IM Motors — SAIC-Alibaba JV, premium smart EVs with solid-state battery plans
  18. Voyah — Dongfeng's premium EV arm, already selling in Norway market
  19. Seres — Huawei-powered AITO brand, advanced smart cockpit and autonomous driving
  20. Xiamen Golden Dragon — Bus and commercial EV specialist, strong in developing markets
