5 Best Interactive Flat Panels for Classrooms in 2026

June 1, 2026 7 min read

Interactive flat panels (IFPs) have become the centerpiece of modern K-12 classrooms, replacing aging projectors and standard whiteboards. These all-in-one touch displays combine high-resolution screens with built-in computing, collaboration software, and robust durability designed for daily student use. Choosing the right model for your school district or individual classroom involves evaluating size, touch technology, software ecosystem, and total cost of ownership. We tested and compared the five leading interactive flat panels available in 2026 to help you make an informed investment.

Stat: According to a 2025 survey by EdTech Digest, 78% of teachers reported that interactive flat panels significantly increased student engagement during lessons, compared to traditional projector setups.

Below we break down the unique strengths of each panel, covering display quality, responsiveness, built-in tools, and integration with existing classroom platforms.

1. ViewSonic IFP7550 - Best Overall for Schools

The ViewSonic IFP7550 is a 75-inch 4K Ultra HD interactive panel that strikes an excellent balance between performance and price. It features 20-point touch recognition, zero-bezel design, and an anti-glare coating that reduces reflections under bright classroom lights. The panel runs ViewSonic's vBoard software, which offers intuitive annotation tools, screen recording, and cloud storage integration with Google Drive and Microsoft OneDrive.

Teachers appreciate the low-latency stylus that mimics pen-on-paper feel, while built-in speakers deliver clear audio for video content without external speakers. The IFP7550 also includes a built-in Android operating system, allowing teachers to use apps directly without connecting a computer. For schools with existing Google Classroom or Microsoft Teams subscriptions, the panel integrates seamlessly. Price point: roughly $3,500-$4,000, making it one of the most cost-effective options in its class.

One limitation is the absence of an integrated camera, but most schools use external webcams for hybrid lessons. The IFP7550 also comes with a three-year warranty and free vBoard software updates, reducing long-term costs.

2. Promethean ActivPanel 9 - Best for Software Ecosystem

Promethean's ActivPanel 9 is the latest iteration of their industry-leading panels, now available in 65-, 75-, and 86-inch sizes. The panel uses advanced Vellum touch technology that provides a paper-like writing experience with exceptional accuracy and palm rejection. The ActivPanel 9 runs ActivInspire and the cloud-based ClassFlow software, which includes lesson delivery tools, real-time student response, and assessment features.

A standout feature is the ActivPanel 9's ability to pair with Promethean's ActivSync device management, allowing IT administrators to remotely update firmware, push apps, and monitor panel health across an entire campus. For schools already invested in Promethean resources, this panel enhances workflow without a learning curve. The 4K display offers 4ms response time and 400 nits brightness, ensuring readability from all angles.

Price starts at $4,200 for the 65-inch model, which is premium but justified by the comprehensive software suite and support. Teacher training resources are extensive, including free webinars and lesson plan libraries.

3. Samsung Flip Pro WM85B - Best for Collaboration and Design

Samsung's Flip Pro WM85B (85-inch) is designed for collaboration-heavy classrooms and meeting spaces. It boasts a sleek, ultra-thin aluminum frame and a 4K 60Hz display with 500 nits brightness, making it visible even in sunlit rooms. The panel supports up to 40 touch points (with appropriate stylus), enabling multiple students to interact simultaneously without lag.

The Flip Pro's software centers on the Samsung Flip app, which allows wireless screen sharing from any device (iOS, Android, Windows, Mac) via Wi-Fi or NFC tap. Teachers can save annotated screens directly to the cloud or export as PDF. The panel also includes a built-in 10MP camera and microphone array for seamless hybrid connectivity, a rare feature among IFPs.

Samsung's commitment to durability includes a reinforced glass surface that resists scratches and accidental impacts. The primary downside is price: the WM85B retails for $5,800-$6,500, making it the most expensive option on this list. However, for schools that prioritize design, wireless flexibility, and hybrid readiness, the investment pays off.

4. SMART Board MX Pro - Best for Established Ecosystems

SMART Technologies remains a trusted name in education, and the MX Pro series (available in 65-, 75-, and 86-inch) continues that legacy. The MX Pro uses SMART's patented HybridTouch technology, which supports both finger and stylus input with 20-point simultaneous touch. The panel runs SMART Notebook, a powerful lesson creation tool used by millions of educators worldwide, and includes Lumio, the cloud-based version for online collaboration.

What sets the MX Pro apart is its built-in SMART amp software, which turns the panel into a collaborative workspace where students can join from their own devices. The panel also features integrated front-facing speakers and a USB-C port for easy wired connectivity. Teachers report that the MX Pro's screen response is highly accurate, even with rapid multi-user input, making it ideal for interactive games and group problem-solving.

The MX Pro includes a 5-year warranty and free SMART Notebook basic licensing, though the premium version requires a subscription. Price ranges from $3,800 to $5,200 depending on size. Schools with existing SMART Board installations will find the MX Pro a natural upgrade path.

5. Newline TruTouch TR75 - Best Value for Budget-Conscious Districts

Newline Interactive's TruTouch TR75 (75-inch) is the best option for schools that need strong core features at a lower price point. Priced around $2,800, it offers 4K resolution, 20-point infrared touch, and an anti-microbial screen coating that helps reduce germ spread in shared environments. The TR75 runs Newline's own operating system alongside Android, and supports wireless screen mirroring via AirPlay, Miracast, and Chromecast built-in.

Newline includes a 7-year warranty on the panel, which is exceptional in the industry. The panel comes with a basic annotation tool and limited pre-installed apps, but teachers can add more via the Google Play Store. For districts buying in bulk, Newline offers volume discounts and a simple deployment process with no licensing fees for core software.

The trade-offs: the touch response is slightly less precise than the Promethean or SMART, and the built-in speakers are adequate but not powerful. However, for schools prioritizing budget without sacrificing basic functionality, the TruTouch TR75 delivers remarkable value.

Final Considerations for Your Purchase

When selecting an interactive flat panel, consider not just the hardware but the total cost over three to five years. Factoring in software subscriptions, installation, training, and potential peripherals (e.g., stylus replacements, mounts) will give you a clearer picture. All five panels reviewed here meet high standards for clarity, interactivity, and durability. We recommend requesting a demo from your top two choices before committing to a large order.

For most K-12 schools, the ViewSonic IFP7550 offers the best combination of features, ease of use, and price. If your district already uses Promethean or SMART tools, stick with that ecosystem to avoid retraining. For cutting-edge design and hybrid readiness, the Samsung Flip Pro is unmatched. And for tight budgets, the Newline TruTouch is a solid, long-warranty choice.
