5 Best Smart Home Energy-Saving Devices for Your Home in 2026

May 26, 2026 7 min read

1. Smart Thermostats: The Cornerstone of Home Energy Efficiency

A smart thermostat is perhaps the most powerful single upgrade you can make for energy savings. Devices like the Nest Learning Thermostat and Ecobee SmartThermostat use advanced sensors, occupancy detection, and machine learning to optimize your heating and cooling schedule automatically. According to the U.S. Department of Energy, a smart thermostat can save homeowners up to 10% annually on heating and cooling costs, which translates to roughly $140 to $180 per year for an average household.

Beyond simple scheduling, modern smart thermostats integrate with your HVAC system to provide remote access via smartphone apps, geofencing that adjusts temperature when you leave or return, and detailed energy usage reports. The Ecobee SmartThermometer even comes with room sensors that help eliminate hot and cold spots. Look for models with ENERGY STAR certification to maximize rebate eligibility and proven savings. Installation typically takes under an hour if you have a C-wire, but many older homes may require a simple adapter kit included with most units.

2. Smart Lighting Systems: Control Every Bulb for Greater Efficiency

Smart lighting goes far beyond turning lights on and off with your voice. Systems like Philips Hue and LIFX allow you to create schedules, adjust brightness, and change colors from anywhere. Energy savings come from automation: lights that turn off automatically when no one is in the room, dim based on natural light, or fade out at bedtime. Replacing standard LEDs (which already use 75% less energy than incandescents) with smart bulbs adds an additional layer of control that typically cuts lighting energy use by another 15% to 20%.

Installing smart lighting is straightforward--screw in a bulb and connect to the app. Most systems work with voice assistants like Alexa, Google Assistant, or Siri. For whole-home control, consider smart switches instead of bulbs for fixtures that require multiple bulbs or dimmer compatibility. The Philips Hue White Ambiance is an excellent entry point, offering tunable white temperatures from warm to cool, helping you align lighting with your circadian rhythm while saving energy. Pair with motion sensors for even greater hands-off savings.

3. Smart Plugs: Turn Dumb Devices into Energy-Saving Gadgets

Smart plugs are the cheapest way to reduce standby power consumption, also known as vampire power, which accounts for up to 10% of home electricity use. Devices like TP-Link Kasa Smart Plug and Amazon Smart Plug let you remotely control any plugged-in appliance--lamps, fans, coffee makers, or entertainment systems--and schedule them to turn off when not in use.

For example, set your home office printer and monitor to turn off at 10 PM and back on at 8 AM. Smart plugs with energy monitoring, like the Kasa KP125, show you real-time wattage and historical consumption, enabling you to identify which devices are energy hogs. Many smart plugs also support away modes that randomly turn lights on and off to simulate occupancy, improving home security without extra cost. With an average price of $10 to $25 per plug, the upfront investment pays back in energy savings within a few months.

4. Smart Irrigation Controllers: Water Your Landscape Efficiently

Outdoor water use accounts for nearly 30% of household water consumption, and much of it is wasted due to inefficient sprinkler scheduling or overwatering. Smart irrigation controllers like the Rachio 3 and Orbit B-hyve use local weather data, soil moisture sensors, and zone-specific plant type information to deliver exactly the right amount of water at the right time. The EPA reports that smart controllers can reduce outdoor water use by 20% to 50%, saving up to 8,800 gallons per year for a typical suburban lawn.

Installation is usually a simple one-for-one swap of your existing timer, connecting it to Wi-Fi. The companion app lets you set schedules, adjust zones for grass, flower beds, or shrubs, and even skip watering when rain is forecast. Some models integrate with Alexa or Google Assistant for voice commands. Over a single season, the water savings often cover the cost of the controller (typically $150 to $250). If your area has water restrictions or drought conditions, this upgrade is both eco-friendly and financially smart.

5. Smart Energy Monitors: See Your Usage in Real-Time

Knowledge is power when it comes to energy savings. Smart energy monitors like the Sense and Emporia Vue give you a detailed, real-time breakdown of exactly what each appliance is consuming. The Sense monitor uses machine learning to identify individual devices--from your refrigerator to your water heater to your toaster--and shows you their energy draw in an intuitive app. Emporia Vue offers flexible sensor clamps for both whole-home and circuit-level monitoring.

Studies show that households with real-time energy feedback reduce their electricity use by an average of 12%. The savings come from immediate behavioral changes: turning off equipment that is still running, replacing inefficient appliances, and scheduling heavy loads during off-peak hours. Most monitors install directly in your main electrical panel (if you are comfortable with basic electrical work, or hire an electrician for $100-$200), and provide year-over-year comparisons so you can track improvements. The Emporia Vue 2 even includes a solar monitoring option if you have or plan to install PV panels. With prices ranging from $100 to $300, these devices pay for themselves within two to three years through reduced bills.
