Top 10 Chinese Electric Motorcycle Brands Reshaping Global Urban Mobility in 2026

May 24, 2026 8 min read

1. NIU: The Global Smart Scooter Pioneer

NIU Technologies remains the undisputed leader in premium smart electric scooters, with over 3 million units sold globally by early 2026. Founded in 2014 and listed on NASDAQ, NIU has established a presence in 50+ countries, including strong footholds in Europe, Southeast Asia, and North America. The brand's flagship models--the NIU NQi GT and MQi GT EVO--combine Bosch motors, lithium-ion battery packs with 70-90 mile ranges, and the NIU App for GPS tracking, remote lock/unlock, and ride analytics.

NIU's competitive advantage lies in its vertically integrated battery ecosystem. The company operates battery-swapping stations in select cities and offers portable batteries that charge in under 4 hours. Pricing ranges from $1,500 for entry-level models to $4,500 for the performance-oriented NQi GT. For commuters who value connectivity and design, NIU is the benchmark.

Key Stat: NIU sold over 1.2 million units in 2025 alone, with 40% of revenue coming from international markets--making it the most globally recognized Chinese EV two-wheeler brand.

2. Yadea: The Volume King with Premium Ambitions

Yadea Group Holdings is the world's largest electric two-wheeler manufacturer by volume, shipping over 10 million units annually. Headquartered in Wuxi, China, Yadea dominates the domestic market with a 25% share and exports to 80+ countries. While historically known for budget commuters, Yadea's G5S and G6 models have pushed into premium territory, featuring graphene batteries, 1,200W motors, and TFT displays with smartphone connectivity.

Yadea's key advantage is scale. The company operates 8 manufacturing bases and has the deepest distribution network in China, with over 20,000 retail outlets. Its pricing spans from $400 for basic models to $2,800 for the G6 Max. For cost-conscious buyers seeking reliability and parts availability, Yadea offers unmatched value. The brand is also investing heavily in battery-swapping infrastructure through its Huanhuan Huan subsidiary.

3. Segway-Ninebot: From Personal Transport to Urban Mobility Powerhouse

Segway-Ninebot, a subsidiary of Xiaomi-backed Ninebot Limited, has evolved far beyond its self-balancing scooter roots. The company's E300SE electric scooter is a flagship model with dual motors, 75 mph top speed, and a 90-mile range, targeting performance-minded urban riders. Segway-Ninebot also produces the C-series and D-series for lighter commuting, all integrated with the Segway-Ninebot App for anti-theft, navigation, and firmware updates.

The brand's competitive edge is its deep integration with Xiaomi's ecosystem and its global service network spanning 100+ countries. Segway-Ninebot sold over 2 million units in 2025, with strong presence in Europe and the US. Pricing ranges from $600 for entry-level models to $4,000 for the E300SE. For riders who want cutting-edge tech and performance, Segway-Ninebot is a top contender.

4. Zeeho: CFMoto's Premium Electric Motorcycle Brand

Zeeho is the premium electric motorcycle arm of CFMoto, the Chinese manufacturer known for its partnership with KTM. Launched in 2021, Zeeho has quickly established itself as a serious player with the AE8 model--a high-performance electric scooter featuring a mid-drive motor, 12.5 kW peak power, and a 0-50 mph time of 4.9 seconds. The AE8 uses a 4.2 kWh lithium battery pack and offers a 75-mile range, with fast charging to 80% in 2 hours.

Zeeho's advantage is its motorsports pedigree. CFMoto's experience in Moto3 and collaboration with KTM translates to superior chassis tuning and braking components from Brembo. Zeeho models are priced between $3,500 and $6,000, targeting the premium commuter and enthusiast market. For riders who want a sporty, well-engineered electric scooter that rivals European brands, Zeeho is the choice.

5. Aima: China's Largest EV Two-Wheeler Manufacturer by Revenue

Aima Technology Group is the largest electric two-wheeler manufacturer in China by revenue, with annual sales exceeding 8 million units. Founded in 1999, Aima has a massive domestic network of 30,000+ retail stores and exports to 70+ countries. The brand's M1 and A-series models are popular for daily commuting, featuring lead-acid and lithium battery options, 500W-1,200W motors, and ranges of 40-60 miles.

Aima's key strength is affordability and scale. Its pricing starts at just $300 for basic models, making it accessible to the mass market. However, Aima is also pushing into smart features with its Aima Smart App and GPS anti-theft systems. For budget-conscious buyers in developing markets, Aima offers the best price-to-value ratio. The company is also investing in battery recycling and solar charging stations.

6. Tailing: Long-Range Commuter Specialist

Tailing Electric Vehicle Co., Ltd. has carved a niche as the long-range specialist among Chinese EV two-wheeler brands. The Tailing TL800 model offers a claimed 120-mile range on a single charge, thanks to its 72V 40Ah lithium battery and 1,500W motor. Tailing focuses on practical commuting with features like large under-seat storage, USB charging ports, and LED lighting.

Tailing's competitive advantage is its focus on range and durability. The brand uses automotive-grade battery management systems and offers a 3-year warranty on batteries. Pricing ranges from $800 to $2,000, targeting commuters who need to travel 30+ miles daily. For delivery riders and long-distance commuters, Tailing is a reliable choice.

7. Xiaodao: The Budget Import Favorite

Xiaodao (also known as Xiaodao Electric) has gained popularity among importers and budget-conscious riders in North America and Europe. The Xiaodao XD1200 is a 1,200W electric scooter with a 60-mile range, priced under $1,000. While build quality and after-sales support can be inconsistent, Xiaodao offers remarkable value for the price.

Xiaodao's advantage is its low cost and simplicity. Models use basic lead-acid or lithium batteries, hub motors, and minimal electronics. The brand sells primarily through online channels and importers. For DIY enthusiasts or riders on a tight budget who are willing to handle maintenance themselves, Xiaodao is an entry point into electric mobility.

8. Sunra: The Global Exporter with Diverse Models

Sunra (Jiangsu Sunra Electric Vehicle Co., Ltd.) is one of China's largest electric two-wheeler exporters, shipping to 80+ countries. The brand offers a wide range from 48V commuters to 72V performance scooters, with models like the Sunra Robo and Sunra Hawk. Sunra's key feature is its modular battery system, allowing riders to swap batteries at designated stations.

Sunra's competitive edge is its global service network and compliance with international standards (CE, EEC, DOT). Pricing ranges from $500 to $2,500. For buyers in Europe and Southeast Asia who need a reliable, certified electric scooter with local support, Sunra is a solid choice.

9. Luyuan: The Veteran with Smart Technology

Luyuan Electric Vehicle Co., Ltd. has been manufacturing electric two-wheelers since 1997 and is known for its focus on safety and smart technology. The Luyuan S30 and Luyuan M6 models feature AI-powered anti-theft systems, NFC keyless start, and regenerative braking. Luyuan uses lithium iron phosphate (LFP) batteries for longer life and safety.

Luyuan's advantage is its R&D investment in battery safety and smart features. The brand holds over 1,000 patents and has a strong domestic presence with 15,000+ stores. Pricing ranges from $600 to $2,000. For tech-savvy riders who prioritize safety and smart features, Luyuan is a compelling option.

10. Super Soco: The Retro-Style Urban Performer

Super Soco, a brand under the Vmoto Group, has gained a cult following for its retro-styled electric motorcycles like the TC Max and CUx. The TC Max features a 4,000W motor, 60-mile range, and a top speed of 60 mph, with a design inspired by café racers. Super Soco has a strong presence in Europe, Australia, and Southeast Asia, with over 100,000 units sold globally.

Super Soco's advantage is its unique design language and community engagement. The brand sponsors racing events and has a loyal owner base. Pricing ranges from $2,500 to $5,000. For riders who want an electric motorcycle that looks and feels like a classic gas bike, Super Soco delivers.

Which Brand Should You Choose?

For daily commuting with smart features, NIU or Segway-Ninebot are top picks. For budget-friendly reliability, Yadea or Aima offer the best value. For long-range needs, Tailing excels. For performance and sporty handling, Zeeho is unmatched. For retro style, Super Soco stands out. For import-friendly budget options, Xiaodao works. For global compliance and support, Sunra and Luyuan are safe bets.

The Chinese electric motorcycle industry is no longer just about cheap alternatives--it's a powerhouse of innovation, scale, and quality. As urban mobility shifts toward electrification, these ten brands are leading the charge globally.
