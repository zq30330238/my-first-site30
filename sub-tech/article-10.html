Best Wireless Earbuds Under $100 (2026)

RC

Rachel Chen

May 4, 2026 · 11 min read

The wireless earbud market in 2026 has reached an inflection point. Features that were exclusive to $200-300 flagships just two years ago — active noise cancellation, spatial audio, multipoint Bluetooth, and 8+ hours of battery life — have trickled down to the sub-$100 segment. You no longer need to spend premium money to get a premium listening experience. After testing over 15 budget wireless earbuds, we have identified the five best options that deliver outstanding value across different use cases. Whether you prioritize sound quality, noise cancellation, call performance, or workout durability, there is a perfect pair on this list for you.

What to Look for in Budget Wireless Earbuds

Before diving into our top picks, here are the key criteria that separate great budget earbuds from mediocre ones. Understanding these factors will help you weigh the trade-offs each model makes.

Sound Quality

Almost all budget earbuds now support at least AAC and SBC codecs, with many adding higher-quality options like LDAC and aptX Adaptive. Driver size (typically 10-13mm in this price range) matters less than the tuning. Many budget earbuds now ship with companion apps that include customizable EQ, allowing you to tailor the sound profile to your preference. Look for earbuds that offer a relatively neutral out-of-box tuning — overly bass-heavy or treble-boosted profiles are harder to correct with EQ than a balanced starting point.

Active Noise Cancellation (ANC)

ANC in budget earbuds has improved dramatically. While you will not get the class-leading isolation of Sony WF-1000XM6 or AirPods Pro (3rd gen), the best sub-$100 options now reduce low-frequency hum (airplane engines, air conditioners, traffic rumble) by 25-35dB — enough to make a meaningful difference on commutes and in noisy offices. Look for models that offer adjustable ANC levels and a transparency mode for when you need to hear your surroundings.

Battery Life

Expect at least 6-8 hours of playback from the earbuds themselves, with the charging case providing an additional 3-4 full charges for 24-32 hours total. Fast charging support (10 minutes for 1-2 hours of playback) is now common. Wireless charging on the case remains a premium feature at this price point but is starting to appear on select budget models.

Comfort and Fit

Comfort is subjective, but some objective factors matter: weight (lighter is better for long sessions), the availability of multiple ear tip sizes (at least S/M/L should be included), and the presence of ear wings or fins for stability during workouts. Stem-style earbuds (like AirPods) distribute weight differently than button-style earbuds, and personal preference here matters enormously. If possible, buy from retailers with generous return policies so you can test fit comfortably.

Top 5 Wireless Earbuds Under $100 (2026)

1. Soundcore Liberty 4 NC — Best Overall

Anker's Soundcore brand continues to dominate the budget audio space, and the Liberty 4 NC represents the pinnacle of what is possible under $100. With adaptive ANC that reduces ambient noise by up to 98%, these earbuds rival noise cancellation performance from models costing twice as much. The 11mm drivers deliver rich, detailed sound with the ability to fine-tune via the Soundcore app's HearID personalized EQ, which measures your hearing profile and adjusts accordingly. Battery life is exceptional at 10 hours per charge (ANC on) with an additional 40 hours in the case. Multipoint Bluetooth 5.4 allows seamless switching between two devices, and the IPX4 water resistance handles workouts and light rain without issue.

Best for: All-around users who want the best ANC, sound quality, and battery life in a single package without compromise.

Price: $79-89 (frequently on sale at $69)

2. EarFun Air Pro 4 — Best Sound Quality

If audio quality is your top priority, the EarFun Air Pro 4 punches well above its weight class. These earbuds support the Qualcomm aptX Lossless codec (with compatible phones), delivering CD-quality wireless audio that was previously reserved for $200+ models. The hybrid dual-driver configuration (10mm dynamic driver + balanced armature) produces a well-separated soundstage with crisp highs, present mids, and controlled bass. The ANC is competent though not best-in-class, and call quality benefits from Qualcomm's cVc 8.0 noise suppression. Battery life is 7.5 hours (ANC on) with 30 hours total in the case. The slightly larger earbud size may not suit smaller ears, but the audio performance at this price is unmatched.

Best for: Audiophiles on a budget who prioritize sound quality above all else.

Price: $89-99

3. Samsung Galaxy Buds FE — Best for Samsung Users

Samsung's Galaxy Buds FE (Fan Edition) bring the core Galaxy Buds experience to a more accessible price point. The 12mm drivers deliver Samsung's signature warm, energetic tuning. ANC is effective, especially at blocking constant low-frequency noise. The integration with Samsung phones is seamless — automatic switching between Galaxy devices, Samsung Scalable Codec for higher-quality audio, and SmartThings Find for locating lost buds. Battery life is solid at 8.5 hours (ANC on). The IPX7 water resistance rating is the best on this list, meaning these can withstand submersion in 1 meter of water for 30 minutes — ideal for intense workouts. The main drawback is that some features are exclusive to Samsung devices.

Best for: Samsung phone users and fitness enthusiasts who need extreme water resistance.

Price: $79-99

4. OnePlus Buds 3 — Best for Calls

The OnePlus Buds 3 excel where many budget earbuds struggle: phone and video calls. The triple-microphone array with AI noise reduction isolates your voice remarkably well even in windy or noisy environments. Audio quality from the 10.4mm drivers is crisp and detailed, though the bass response is more restrained than some competitors. ANC performs well, and the transparency mode sounds natural. Battery life is 6.5 hours (ANC on) with 28 hours total — slightly below the class leaders. The stem design is comfortable for extended wear, and Google Fast Pair support makes setup painless on Android. The companion HeyMelody app provides EQ customization and firmware updates.

Best for: Remote workers and anyone who takes frequent calls and needs clear voice quality above all.

Price: $89-99

5. JLab JBuds ANC 3 — Best Ultra-Budget Option

For those who want ANC on the tightest possible budget, the JLab JBuds ANC 3 delivers at just $49-59. The ANC is basic but genuinely functional, reducing engine hum and background chatter noticeably. Sound quality leans bass-forward with JLab's signature "JLab Signature" EQ, and the companion app offers additional EQ presets plus a custom 10-band equalizer. Battery life is a strong 9 hours (ANC on). The charging case includes an integrated USB-C cable, eliminating the need to carry a separate cable. Build quality and materials feel more budget than the other picks, but at this price, what JLab delivers is remarkable. IP55 dust and water resistance provides solid workout protection.

Best for: Maximum value shoppers who want ANC on the absolute cheapest budget possible.

Price: $49-59

Which Budget Earbuds Should You Buy?

Your choice among these five comes down to priorities. The Soundcore Liberty 4 NC is the safest recommendation — it does everything well and nothing poorly, with best-in-class ANC and battery. If sound quality is your primary concern, the EarFun Air Pro 4's aptX Lossless support and dual-driver setup deliver the most detailed audio. Samsung users should strongly consider the Galaxy Buds FE for ecosystem integration and water resistance. If calls dominate your usage, the OnePlus Buds 3 microphone system is the best you will find under $100. And if you simply want ANC on the cheapest possible budget, the JLab JBuds ANC 3 is a genuine achievement at its price point.

The wireless earbud market in 2026 means you truly cannot go wrong at this price point. Each of these five models would have been considered flagship-tier just a few years ago. The democratization of audio technology is one of the best stories in consumer tech, and these earbuds are the proof.

Pro Tip: Whichever earbuds you choose, invest $5-10 in a set of aftermarket memory foam ear tips (Comply or similar). They improve both comfort and passive noise isolation more than any other single variable, and they can transform the fit of earbuds that feel slightly loose or uncomfortable with stock silicone tips.
