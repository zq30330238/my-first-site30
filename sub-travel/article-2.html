How to Find Cheap Flights: Expert Hacks

Master the art of finding affordable airfare with these proven strategies from seasoned travelers and flight hackers.

By TripRoute Editorial Team | May 14, 2026 | 11 min read
Advertisement

Flights are often the single biggest expense of any trip. But savvy travelers know that airline pricing is a complex game — and once you understand the rules, you can consistently find fares far below what most people pay. This guide consolidates years of flight-hacking experience into actionable strategies you can use today.

Part 1: The Right Tools for the Job

Flight Search Engines Compared

Not all flight search engines are created equal. Each has strengths, weaknesses, and blind spots in their coverage. Here is how the major players stack up:

Google Flights — The best starting point for most searches. Its calendar view instantly shows which dates are cheapest, and the "Explore" feature lets you find deals to anywhere on a given date. Google Flights searches most airlines directly but does miss some budget carriers, especially in Asia and Europe. Its price-tracking alerts are the most reliable in the industry.

Skyscanner — The best aggregator for budget airlines worldwide. Skyscanner includes carriers that Google Flights misses, particularly Ryanair, AirAsia, and other low-cost airlines. The "Everywhere" search function is unmatched for flexible-trip inspiration. However, Skyscanner sometimes redirects to third-party booking sites with questionable customer service — always verify before booking.

Momondo — Known for excellent multi-city and complex-route pricing. Momondo's interface is visually appealing, and its "Flight Insight" feature rates whether a fare is good value based on historical data. It also searches some smaller regional OTAs that other engines miss.

Kayak — Strong for comparing across multiple booking sites simultaneously. Kayak's "Hacker Fare" feature automatically combines two one-way tickets on different airlines to create a cheaper round-trip. Its price forecasting tool predicts whether fares will rise or fall in the next 7 days with reasonable accuracy.

Kiwi.com — Unique for its "virtual interlining" feature that combines flights from airlines that do not normally partner. This can create routes that are significantly cheaper than booking directly. However, Kiwi's self-transfer guarantee is limited, and their customer service has mixed reviews.

The Multi-Engine Strategy

Experienced flight hackers never rely on a single search engine. The optimal approach: start with Google Flights to understand the route and pricing landscape, cross-reference on Skyscanner for budget airlines, check Momondo for multi-city alternatives, and then compare the final price directly on the airline's own website before booking. This four-step process takes 15-20 minutes but routinely saves $50-200 per ticket.

Part 2: When and How to Book

The Best Time to Book

Contrary to the persistent myth of "book on Tuesday at 3am," the optimal booking window depends on the route. For domestic flights within the US, the sweet spot is typically 1-3 months before departure. For international flights, the window shifts to 2-8 months out, with 4-6 months being ideal for most long-haul routes.

Peak seasons dramatically shift these windows. If you are traveling during Christmas, summer holidays, or major festivals, book as early as possible — prices for these dates almost never go down. For shoulder-season and off-peak travel, waiting for a sale (even 4-6 weeks before departure) can pay off.

Flexible Date Strategies

The single most powerful tool for saving money on flights is date flexibility. A departure one day earlier or later often means hundreds of dollars in savings. Google Flights' calendar view and Skyscanner's "whole month" search make this easy to visualize.

Key rules for date flexibility: mid-week flights (Tuesday, Wednesday) are consistently cheaper than weekend flights. Red-eye and early-morning departures tend to be the cheapest time slots. For international travel, Tuesday and Wednesday departures can be 15-30% cheaper than Friday-Sunday departures on many routes.

Also consider the "shoulder season" strategy: traveling in the 2-3 weeks immediately before or after peak season often yields the same weather at a fraction of the price. For Europe, this means May or late September instead of July-August. For Southeast Asia, aim for November or February instead of December-January.

Hidden City Ticketing Explained

Hidden city ticketing — also known as skiplagging — involves booking a flight with a layover at your actual destination and simply not boarding the second leg. For example, if you want to fly from New York to Chicago, you might book a New York-to-Denver flight that connects through Chicago for less than the direct New York-to-Chicago fare, and simply exit at O'Hare.

This strategy works because airlines price routes based on market competition, not distance. A connecting flight through a competitive hub can paradoxically cost less than a direct flight to that hub.

Critical caveats: You cannot check bags (they will fly to the final destination). This only works for one-way bookings or the last leg of a round-trip. Airlines actively discourage this practice and may penalize frequent hidden-city users by canceling frequent flyer accounts or, in rare cases, pursuing legal action. Use this tactic sparingly and never on trips where you need checked luggage.

Part 3: Miles, Points, and Advanced Tactics

Maximizing Miles and Points

Frequent flyer miles and credit card points are not just for business travelers. With the right strategy, even occasional travelers can earn enough for a free flight every year or two. The key is focusing on transferable point currencies — Chase Ultimate Rewards, American Express Membership Rewards, and Citi ThankYou Points — which give you the flexibility to transfer to multiple airline partners when award space opens up.

For economy class redemptions, the best value often comes from booking through airline alliances rather than directly. For example, using Avianca LifeMiles (a Star Alliance member) to book United flights often requires fewer miles than booking through United MileagePlus directly. Tools like AwardHacker and Point.me can help identify these sweet spots.

Price Tracking and Alerts

Set up multiple price alerts for routes you are monitoring. Google Flights, Skyscanner, and Hopper all offer tracking features with slightly different algorithms. Hopper's predictive AI is particularly useful — it tells you whether to "buy now" or "wait" based on its analysis of billions of historical price points, claiming 95% accuracy.

Additionally, subscribe to flight deal newsletters and services: Going (formerly Scott's Cheap Flights), Dollar Flight Club, and Secret Flying all surface error fares, flash sales, and genuinely exceptional deals. A premium subscription to one of these services ($49-99/year) can pay for itself many times over with a single booking.

Regional Booking Tricks

Flights are sometimes significantly cheaper when booked in a different currency or through an airline's foreign website. For example, booking a domestic flight within Thailand on Thai Airways' Thai-language site sometimes yields lower fares than the English international site. Similarly, certain South American routes are substantially cheaper when booked through the local version of LATAM's website in Chilean or Colombian pesos.

Use a VPN to check prices from different countries, and always compare the airline's own website price against what OTAs are showing. But balance this against the risk — if you need to make changes, dealing with a foreign-language booking can be challenging.

The Incognito Mode Myth — and What Actually Works

There is a persistent belief that airlines track your searches and raise prices if you search repeatedly. The evidence for this is weak — most price fluctuations are due to dynamic pricing algorithms responding to overall demand, fare class availability, and competitor pricing, not individual user behavior. While using incognito mode does not hurt, it is unlikely to meaningfully affect the prices you see.

What does matter: clearing your search and starting fresh for each trip, checking prices across multiple devices (mobile vs. desktop occasionally show different fares), and being ready to book immediately when you find a great deal — hesitation is the real enemy of cheap flights.

Your Flight-Hacking Checklist

Finding cheap flights is a skill that improves with practice. Start with a multi-engine search using Google Flights and Skyscanner, stay flexible on dates and airports, monitor prices with alerts, and consider creative routing options. Most importantly, when you see a great fare, book it. The best deals often disappear within hours, and the single biggest mistake travelers make is waiting too long.
