5 Budget-Friendly US Road Trips That Don't Skimp on Scenery

May 23, 2026 7 min read

Road trips are the ultimate way to explore the US, but many travelers assume they're too expensive. The truth is, with strategic planning, you can enjoy stunning landscapes, local culture, and memorable experiences without breaking the bank. Below are five budget-friendly road trips that prioritize scenery and savings, perfect for solo travelers, couples, or families.

1. Blue Ridge Parkway (North Carolina to Virginia)

Stretching 469 miles from Asheville, NC, to Shenandoah National Park, VA, the Blue Ridge Parkway is a budget traveler's dream--there are no tolls, and most overlooks, hiking trails, and scenic stops are completely free. The parkway winds through rolling mountains, vibrant fall foliage, and wildflower meadows, offering postcard-worthy views at every turn.

When it comes to accommodations, you have plenty of low-cost options:

  • Free dispersed camping: Pisgah National Forest allows free, no-reservation camping in designated areas, just a short drive from the parkway.
  • Budget motels: Asheville's downtown area has motels starting at $60 per night, with easy access to the parkway's southern entrance.
  • State park campsites: Linville Gorge Wilderness offers campsites for $22 per night, with access to some of the parkway's best hiking trails.

For food, skip expensive restaurants and opt for local gems: Asheville's River Arts District has food trucks serving $10-$12 meals, while Boone's The Skillet diner offers hearty breakfasts for under $8. Packing a cooler with snacks and water will also help you save on mid-drive stops.

2. Route 66 (Oklahoma to New Mexico Stretch)

While the full Route 66 cross-country trip can be pricey, the Oklahoma to New Mexico segment offers affordable nostalgia and stunning desert scenery without the crowds of the California end. This 400-mile stretch is packed with free and low-cost attractions that capture the spirit of America's Mother Road.

Must-see budget stops include:

  • Cadillac Ranch (Amarillo, TX): A free outdoor art installation where you can spray-paint old Cadillacs half-buried in the ground.
  • Route 66 Museum (Clinton, OK): Entry is just $5, and it features exhibits on the highway's history and cultural impact.
  • El Rancho Hotel & Motel (Gallup, NM): A historic Route 66 motel with rooms starting at $75 per night, perfect for a retro-themed stay.

Food options are equally affordable: Amarillo's Tower Drive-In serves classic burgers for $12, while Santa Fe's local Mexican spots offer happy hour tacos for $5 each. For camping, Oklahoma's Lake Murray State Park has sites for $20 per night, with access to hiking and fishing.

3. Pacific Coast Highway (Monterey to San Luis Obispo)

This 100-mile stretch of California's Pacific Coast Highway (PCH) offers dramatic ocean views, rugged cliffs, and charming coastal towns--all without the high prices of San Francisco or Los Angeles. It's ideal for a 2-3 day road trip, with plenty of free and low-cost activities.

Free stops you won't want to miss:

  • Bixby Creek Bridge (Big Sur): One of the PCH's most iconic landmarks, with free parking and photo opportunities.
  • Monterey Bay Coastal Trail: A 18-mile paved trail along the ocean, perfect for walking, biking, or birdwatching.
  • Morro Rock (Morro Bay): A volcanic plug that's a popular spot for hiking and sunset viewing, with free access.

Accommodations include Andrew Molera State Park campsites for $35 per night, or budget hotels in San Luis Obispo starting at $80 per night. For food, Tognazzini's Dockside in Morro Bay serves fresh fish tacos for $15, while Monterey's weekly farmers market offers affordable produce for packed lunches.

AAA reports that the average cost of a 5-day road trip for a family of four is $1,935, but budget-focused trips can cut that by 40% or more with strategic planning, such as camping instead of staying in hotels and packing meals instead of eating out for every meal.

4. Great River Road (Minneapolis to Dubuque)

Stretching 300 miles along the Upper Mississippi River, the Great River Road offers a mix of natural beauty, historic sites, and small-town charm--all on a budget. The route winds through bluffs, forests, and riverfront towns, with plenty of free and low-cost activities.

Budget-friendly highlights:

  • Great River Bluffs State Park (MN): Free overlooks with panoramic views of the Mississippi River, plus hiking trails for all skill levels.
  • Dubuque Museum of Art (IA): Free entry on Thursdays, with exhibits featuring regional and national artists.
  • La Crosse Riverfront: Free concerts and events during the summer, plus riverfront parks for picnicking.

Accommodations include Mississippi Palisades State Park campsites for $28 per night, or budget hotels in La Crosse starting at $70 per night. For food, City Brew Hall in La Crosse offers happy hour beers for $4, while Minneapolis's Bluebird Diner serves fluffy pancakes for $9.

5. Utah's Mighty 5 Loop (Budget-Friendly Version)

Utah's Mighty 5--Zion, Bryce Canyon, Arches, Canyonlands, and Capitol Reef National Parks--are some of the most stunning landscapes in the US, but peak-season prices can add up. However, visiting in the off-season (spring or fall) and using budget hacks can make this trip affordable.

Key budget tips for the Mighty 5:

  • Buy an America the Beautiful Pass: For $80 per year, you get free entry to all national parks and federal recreational lands--saving you $35 per park entry.
  • Camp on BLM land: Bureau of Land Management areas near Arches and Canyonlands offer free, no-reservation camping, just a short drive from the parks.
  • Pack your own meals: Grocery stores in Moab are affordable, and packing snacks and meals will help you avoid expensive park concessions.

Off-season accommodations in Moab start at $85 per night, and many park trails are free to hike--no need for paid tours. Bryce Canyon's Sunrise Point and Zion's Angels Landing are both free to access, offering some of the most iconic views in the state.

Whether you're looking for mountain scenery, coastal views, or desert landscapes, these budget road trips prove you don't need to spend a fortune to explore the US. By planning ahead, choosing affordable accommodations, and prioritizing free activities, you can create memorable adventures without breaking the bank.
