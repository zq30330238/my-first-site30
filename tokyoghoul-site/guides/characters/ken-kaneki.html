Ken Kaneki

The One-Eyed King / Haise Sasaki

Role

Protagonist

Affiliation

Anteiku / CCG (as Haise)

Kagune

Rinkaku

“It's not that I'm weak. I just don't want to fight. But if I have to fight, I'll fight.”

Overview

Ken Kaneki is the main protagonist of Tokyo Ghoul. A quiet college student who loves reading, his life changes forever after a chance encounter with Rize Kamishiro. Following a traumatic organ transplant, he becomes the first artificial one-eyed ghoul. Kaneki's journey explores his struggle to maintain his humanity while embracing his ghoul powers. He later adopts the alias Haise Sasaki while working with the CCG as an investigator with fragmented memories.

Appearance

Kaneki is a young man with black hair styled in spikes and distinctive dark eyes. After his transformation, his hair gradually turns white due to psychological trauma and RC cell buildup. He is often seen wearing his signature green jacket or, as Haise Sasaki, a CCG investigator suit. His most striking feature is his crimson-red ghoul eye that activates when using his Kagune.

Personality

Initially timid, kind-hearted, and passive, Kaneki is deeply affected by his transformation into a half-ghoul. He develops a split personality: his gentle nature warring with a darker, more aggressive persona born from trauma. As Haise Sasaki, he is charismatic and competent but haunted by suppressed memories. His core trait is his extraordinary will to protect those he cares about.

Abilities

Kaneki possesses a Rinkaku-type Kagune with four distinctive tentacle-like appendages. His Kagune is exceptionally powerful due to his half-human, half-ghoul nature and his RC cell count being off the charts. He develops numerous techniques including the Centipede Kagune form and later achieves the Dragon form, becoming one of the most powerful beings in the series.

Trivia

Kaneki's favorite author is Sen Takatsuki, whose books foreshadow his own fate. His habit of cracking his knuckles is a nervous tic that becomes more pronounced as his mental state destabilizes. The number 12 (his birthday, December 20) appears frequently in the series. His character arc is often analyzed as a representation of trauma-induced personality change.
