Vandal Mastery: Recoil Control and One-Tap Strategies

April 7, 2025 · 6 min read

Understanding the Vandal's Recoil Pattern

The Vandal is Valorant's high-damage assault rifle, capable of one-tapping any enemy with a headshot at any range. But its punishing recoil makes it unforgiving for players who spray without control. The gun's recoil pattern pulls straight up for the first 6-7 bullets, then drifts left and right in an S-curve. To master the Vandal, you need to internalize this pattern. Practice at the range: fire 4-5 bullets without moving your mouse, then reset. The first 4 bullets have minimal spread, making burst fire your best friend. After that, the spray becomes chaotic. Key takeaway: Never commit to a full spray beyond 10 bullets unless the enemy is point-blank. Instead, learn to reset your recoil by releasing the trigger and re-aiming.

Mastering the One-Tap and Burst Fire

The Vandal's one-tap potential is its biggest strength. With a headshot multiplier of 2.5x, a single bullet to the dome kills any agent instantly. To consistently land those one-taps, focus on crosshair placement at head height. Pre-aim common angles and strafe into your shots--stop moving, then fire. Burst fire (2-3 rounds) is the optimal trade-off between speed and accuracy. For mid-range duels, use a short burst aimed at the neck; the second bullet often lands a headshot due to mild vertical recoil. When fighting long-range, tap fire slowly--one click per second. This minimizes spread and keeps your first bullet accurate. Remember: the Vandal's first shot is 100% accurate, even while moving if you counter-strafe correctly.

Positioning and Economy Management

The Vandal costs 2900 credits, making it a mid-tier rifle. You'll rarely buy it on eco rounds; instead, save for a Phantom if you prefer a more forgiving spray. But on full-buy rounds, the Vandal rewards players who hold tight angles and peek with precision. Avoid taking long spray battles against players using the Phantom at close range--the Phantom's higher fire rate and lower spread win there. Use the Vandal when you want to punish enemies holding exposed angles or when you need to one-tap through smoke (since the Vandal has no tracers for the enemy). For defense, pair the Vandal with a Sheriff or Classic on save rounds; for attack, use a utility-heavy agent like Omen or Breach to create space for your precision shots.

Pro Tip: Crosshair placement is everything with the Vandal. Keep your crosshair at head level as you move through the map. Even a split-second adjustment can cost you the fight. Practice the 'deathmatch' mode with only Vandal headshots to build muscle memory.

Finally, manage your economy. If you have 3900 credits, buy Vandal + Heavy Shields. If you're low, skip the shield and rely on your aim. Never buy a Vandal without armor unless you are absolutely certain you won't take damage. The Vandal's strength is its one-shot kill potential; a headshot is meaningless if you're already dead from a body spray. Stay disciplined, control your bursts, and the Vandal will reward you with consistent, satisfying kills.
