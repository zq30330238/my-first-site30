Post-Plant Strategy: How to Lock Down Rounds After Planting

March 26, 2025 · 5 min read

Controlling Angles

Once the spike is down, your team's priority shifts from planting to holding space. Stop playing for entry. Instead, position yourself to cover the defuse area while staying alive. Pick one or two strong sightlines and commit to them. Avoid spreading out too far--if you die isolated, the enemy gets free numbers. On maps like Bind, holding U-Hall or short while a teammate watches back site forces attackers to push through narrow chokepoints. On Haven, garage and A long work together to cover the spike from two directions. Communicate which angles you own and rotate only when you hear the defuse or see utility.

Crossfires and Utility

Crossfires are the backbone of post-plant defense. When two teammates aim at the same defuse zone from different angles, the enemy cannot peek without taking fire from both sides. For example, on Ascent B, one player watches from market while another holds back site. The defuser must choose which angle to clear, but clearing one exposes them to the other. Use utility to delay or punish the defuse. Phoenix's Molly, Killjoy's Nanoswarm, and Brimstone's Incendiary force attackers off the spike or buy time. Save these abilities for after the plant. Deploy them after you hear the defuse sound--most players commit and take damage before backing off. Bait out their utility first. If the enemy has a Skye or Sova, expect recon bolts or dog to clear corners. Wait for those to expire before sitting on angle.

Time Management

Post-plant rounds often come down to seconds. Know the spike timer: 45 seconds from plant, 7 seconds to defuse. If you are up in numbers, do not peek early. Let the clock pressure the attackers. If you are down a player, you may need to take a risky fight to prevent a safe defuse. Use the spike timer as a weapon. If the enemy is defusing with less than 5 seconds left and you are far away, save your gun and play for the next round--the spike will explode. When you are the last defender, prioritize staying alive over getting kills. A single contact kill buys 3-4 seconds of delay. If you are low on health, hide until the defuse starts, then jiggle peek to interrupt without committing. Post-plant is not about fragging; it is about wasting the enemy's time.

Pro tip: When the spike is planted and you are low on health, hide until you hear the defuse sound. Do not peek early--wait for the enemy to commit, then jiggle out to shoot them while they are stationary. One bullet can save the round.
