Our Mission

Myers Architecture was founded with a simple mission: to make architectural knowledge accessible and engaging for everyone. We believe that understanding the built environment enriches our lives, deepens our appreciation of history and culture, and empowers us to make better decisions about our own spaces.

Architecture is too important to leave to architects alone. Every building we enter, every street we walk, every room we inhabit shapes our experience of the world. Understanding how and why buildings are designed the way they are gives us a new lens for seeing our surroundings.

Our team combines architectural expertise with a passion for clear, engaging writing. We research each topic thoroughly, drawing on scholarly sources while writing for a general audience. We aim to be accurate, insightful, and inspiring.

What We Cover

Our content spans the full range of architectural history and practice. We cover historical styles and movements, from Classical Greek to contemporary Deconstructivism. We explore building types from around the world, from European castles to Japanese single-family homes.

Our interior design section helps readers understand the principles behind popular aesthetics. Our landmark pages dive deep into the world's most famous buildings. Our comparison pages offer thought-provoking analysis of different architectural traditions.

We are constantly expanding our coverage. New articles are added regularly, and existing articles are updated to reflect new scholarship and perspectives. We welcome suggestions for topics you would like to see covered.

Our Values

We are committed to accuracy, clarity, and intellectual honesty. We cite our sources and distinguish between established facts and interpretation. We aim to represent diverse perspectives and avoid cultural bias in our analysis of non-Western architectural traditions.

We believe architecture is a humanistic discipline that connects to history, culture, technology, and the arts. Our articles place buildings in their full context, exploring not just how they look but what they mean and how they work.

Myers Architecture is part of the Myers Media Network, a collection of information websites dedicated to providing high-quality, accessible content on a range of topics.
